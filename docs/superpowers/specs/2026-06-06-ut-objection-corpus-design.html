UT Objection & Myth Corpus — Design Spec

Date: 2026-06-06  ·  Status: Approved design, pre-implementation  ·  Owner: EngineerDad (Shoo Kyuk Wei)  ·  Schema: ADR-029 knowledge entries

Purpose. A grounded, brand-safe corpus family that answers the objections and myths which keep broad-base Malaysians from investing through Public Mutual unit trusts (UT) and PRS. Built for content generation, not as advice. Thesis: investment is a necessity. Every figure must be factual and cited — no numbers from model memory.

1. Audience & thesis

The audience is broad-base Malaysians — young earners, the self-employed, EPF members, fence-sitters — not parents alone. Throughout this corpus, "the saver" means whichever Malaysian the entry is converting. The underlying conviction is that investing is a necessity rather than a luxury, and the content's job is to dismantle the beliefs that prevent it.

2. Posture — steelman, then segment

Every myth entry concedes the part of the objection that is genuinely true, then introduces the variable the objection ignores, then names the saver-type for whom the answer actually changes. This is the only posture compatible with a data-driven brand: it earns trust by admitting where the alternative wins before carving out where UT/PRS legitimately fits. The three honest carve-outs that recur are (a) the tax-relief wrapper (PRS RM3,000), (b) Shariah depth, and (c) the behaviour gap for savers who will not self-manage.

3. The three-tier funnel (the spine)

Myths sort into three tiers by what they block. This maps onto a marketing funnel (TOFU→MOFU→BOFU) and onto the corpus funnel_tier frontmatter field.

TierQuestion it answersWho it convertsReach
NecessityWhy invest at all?The non-investorBroadest (TOFU)
AvoidanceWhy is UT specifically safe / legit / accessible?The UT-hesitantMid (MOFU)
SubstitutionWhy not just DIY ETF / robo / i-Invest / direct stocks?The already-investing DIY-erNarrowest, most sophisticated (BOFU)

Rationale.

  • Flat, not nested. The indexer (packages/corpus/src/reindex.ts) recurses and derives scope from the top-level dir only, so a subfolder would index fine — but a lone objections/ directory would break parity with the 4 existing flat families. Flat keeps one mental model for humans and the indexer alike.
  • Tier digit in the filename. d0/d1/d2 makes ls corpus/knowledge/d* print TOFU→BOFU, mirroring funnel_tier in frontmatter for redundancy.
  • cluster: objection. A new cluster value (current enum: mechanics | tax | portfolio | primitive) is the machine-readable join brief-writer uses for proof selection; the filename stays human-scannable.
  • Lean on owned proof via related:. Each d- entry links "down" to the grounded mechanics/proof entry it rests on, rather than duplicating facts.

6. Entry template (ADR-029)

One myth = one entry. Frontmatter adds one new field, funnel_tier; everything else is the existing schema.

---
cluster: objection
funnel_tier: necessity | avoidance | substitution
granularity: concept            # or "scenario" for a worked numeric walk-through
source_type: public | synthesized
source_ref: "<citation(s) for every figure used>"
verified_at: YYYY-MM-DD
lang_status: en_only            # BM pass later, per doctrine
related: [<owned-proof-entry>, ...]
---

# <Myth, stated as the saver would say it>

## English
### The myth
### The true part            (steelman concession)
### The variable it ignores   (the turn — every figure cited)
### Who it changes the answer for   (segment)

Bilingual EN/BM is doctrine, but entries draft EN-first and carry lang_status: en_only until the BM pass — identical to the existing PMB entries.

8. Dataset & chart propagation (claims binding)

Each grounded fact ships not only as prose but as a reusable dataset + chart so future content generation has a claim already bound to data. The binding follows the existing pattern in corpus/data/charts/*.yaml: a header Source: line pointing to the dataset, and a header Used by … when scene argues "<hook>" line that is the binding the generator reads.

Binding = 3 artifacts per claim

  • Dataset JSON (corpus/data/*.json) — verified, with source / verified_at; no model-memory numbers.
  • Chart YAML (corpus/data/charts/*.yaml) — header Source: → its dataset; header Used by … "<myth hook>" → the binding; bilingual caption_en/ms + source_citation; semantic_role per series (hero / comparison / floor).
  • proof-asset-index.md row — maps the asset to the claims it backs; each d- myth entry cites the chart id in its body and related:.

10. Out of scope (YAGNI / compliance)

  • No personalised allocation advice. Entries describe mechanics & trade-offs, never "you should buy X." Phrasing governed by compliance-claim-phrasebank.md (FIMM/SC line).
  • No fabricated or model-memory numbers. Any ✗ stays a placeholder until the grounding pass cites it.
  • No full ASB / FD / crypto head-to-heads. They appear only as foils to rebut, not as scored alternatives.
  • No live ETF price quoting. Use dated factsheet figures with the snapshot caveat, like the fund entries.
