# Hypothetical Case Study — The Engineer Dad Archetype

> Persona-anchored hypothetical for `engineer_dad_archetype` — the brand mirror. The analytical professional dad who treats portfolio design like a system. All figures illustrative; no real client data.

## English

### Setup (hypothetical)
- Engineering professional, age 38. Two kids: a 7-year-old and a 4-year-old.
- Household income: ~RM12,000 / month combined (dual-income).
- Existing position: maxed EPF contributions, RM30k in FD, no children's-fund accounts yet.
- Pain point: knows the formula, has run a spreadsheet, but has not yet picked the actual funds. The block is execution, not understanding.

### Plan (hypothetical)
- Two separate children's-fund accounts (one per child) — the goals and timelines are different and shouldn't be co-mingled.
- Allocations:
  - Child 1 (age 7, 11-year horizon): RM300/month, equity-tilt with a glide to lower-volatility funds in years 8–11.
  - Child 2 (age 4, 14-year horizon): RM250/month, equity-tilt for years 1–10, glide in years 11–14.
- Personal leg: RM250/month into PRS (RM3,000/year cap for the tax-relief mechanic), on top of EPF.
- Liquid: keep RM30k FD as 4–6 month emergency reserve; review quarterly to keep the buffer at target.
- All auto-debits date 2 days after salary lands. Annual review only.

### Math (illustrative)
- Child 1: ~RM300/month × 11 years × 6.5% ≈ ~RM63,000 at age 18.
- Child 2: ~RM250/month × 14 years × 7% ≈ ~RM72,000 at age 18.
- PRS: ~RM250/month × N years until age 55, plus tax relief running through 2030.
- Combined: meaningful ground covered toward "Adequate" EPF tier on the retirement leg, plus two funded children's positions on the education leg.

### What this case anchors

The brand-mirror archetype is the most common reader of EngineerDad content. The case demonstrates:
- **Two-account discipline** — don't co-mingle children's funds. Each child has its own horizon.
- **The retirement leg, separately** — children's-fund work doesn't substitute for the parent's own retirement work. PRS sits alongside.
- **Spreadsheet → execution gap** — the brand's whole business is closing this gap, and this archetype is the canonical case.
- **Annual review cadence** — not monthly. Engineer instinct is to monitor; brand counter-pressure is to set, audit, look away.

### Hard rules
- All numbers illustrative, using flat assumed returns.
- Public Mutual fund-specific projections must come from the fund's own Master Prospectus and PHS.
- This case study is hypothetical and does not represent any specific client outcome.

## Bahasa Malaysia

### Persediaan (hipotetikal)
- Profesional kejuruteraan, umur 38. Dua anak: usia 7 dan 4.
- Pendapatan isi rumah: ~RM12,000 / bulan gabungan.
- Posisi sedia ada: caruman EPF maksimum, RM30k dalam FD, tiada akaun dana anak lagi.
- Titik kesakitan: tahu formula, sudah jalankan spreadsheet, tetapi belum pilih dana sebenar. Halangan ialah pelaksanaan.

### Pelan (hipotetikal)
- Dua akaun dana anak berasingan (satu setiap anak).
- Peruntukan:
  - Anak 1 (usia 7, jangka 11 tahun): RM300/bulan.
  - Anak 2 (usia 4, jangka 14 tahun): RM250/bulan.
- Kaki peribadi: RM250/bulan ke PRS (had RM3,000/tahun untuk mekanik pelepasan cukai).
- Cair: simpan RM30k FD sebagai rizab kecemasan 4–6 bulan.

### Apa yang ditambat oleh kes ini
- **Disiplin dua-akaun** — jangan campurkan dana anak.
- **Kaki persaraan, berasingan** — kerja dana anak tidak menggantikan kerja persaraan ibu bapa sendiri.
- **Jurang spreadsheet → pelaksanaan** — keseluruhan perniagaan jenama ialah menutup jurang ini.
