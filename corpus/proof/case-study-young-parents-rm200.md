# Hypothetical Case Study — Young Parents, RM200/month from Birth

> Persona-anchored hypothetical for `young_parents_25_35`. All figures illustrative; no real client data. Use as a worked example for the canonical "RM200/month from birth → RM100k+ at age 18" hook.

## English

### Setup (hypothetical)
- Parents in their late twenties, first child just born.
- Household income: RM6,500 / month combined, modest savings, no existing children's-fund position.
- Goal: fund a local Malaysian degree at age 18.
- Constraint: RM200/month is what they can sustain on an *average* month, not their best month.
- Risk profile: can hold equity-tilt for an 18-year horizon.

### Plan (hypothetical)
- Initial deposit: RM1,000.
- Auto-debit: RM200/month, set 2 days after the elder parent's salary lands.
- Fund category: equity-tilt unit trust, geographically diversified (domestic + Asia-Pac + global blend), Shariah-compliance per family preference. The actual fund choice is made one-on-one with the licensed UTC after a Suitability Assessment Form (SAF) — this case study does not name a specific fund.
- Glide: equity-tilt for years 1–14; gradual shift to lower-volatility funds in years 15–18.
- Review: annually only.

### Math (illustrative)
- 18 years × RM200/month + initial RM1,000 + 8% assumed annual return ≈ RM100,000+ at end of horizon.
- Compare: same plan started at age 5 (13 years of compounding) ≈ RM57,000.
- The 5 missing years of compounding cost ~RM43,000 of end-of-horizon value.

### What this case anchors in content
- The "starting age matters more than amount" frame.
- The "RM200/month is NOT too small" rebuttal.
- The "average-month, not best-month" tactical rule.
- The Latency Penalty as an action item, not a moral.

### Hard rules
- All numbers illustrative, using a flat assumed return. Real fund returns vary year to year.
- Public Mutual fund-specific projections must come from the fund's own Master Prospectus and PHS.
- This case study is hypothetical and does not represent any specific client outcome.
