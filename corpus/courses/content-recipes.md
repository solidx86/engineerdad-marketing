### Structural patterns (the recurring anatomy of any post)

Every educational unit follows the same scaffold:
1. **Surface the bug** — name the wrong assumption most people are operating under.
2. **Show the data** — a stat, a chart, a survey result, a real-money example.
3. **Explain the mechanism** — define the technical concept (DCA, alpha, compounding) in plain English.
4. **Map to action** — exactly what to click, what number to plug in, who to call.
5. **Close with the long-horizon reminder** — bring it back to the 18-year window.

Recurring framing devices for the hook:
- **The data hook**: "83% of you fear losing money in the market…" (uses survey result as opener)
- **The personal anecdote bridge**: "My wife and I recently audited our own household system…" / "In my past life leading software engineering teams…"
- **The reframe**: "Most people think X. The data says Y."
- **The CTA close**: "Swipe to debug your [X]." / "Hit follow for Part [N]." / "DM me 'REVIEW' for a free portfolio review."

### The 5-Step Education Fund Playbook (the canonical how-to template)

Source: engineerdad.my/articles/start-childrens-education-fund-malaysia.html. This is the structure for any new "how to" article.

1. **Define the goal in numbers.** Three inputs: time horizon · target amount (RM100k–150k local; 2–3× for overseas) · monthly capacity (average month, not best month). Plug into the EngineerDad compounding tool at 7–8% assumed return.
2. **Pick the right fund category.** Three axes: asset class (equity-heavy for long horizons, more conservative as the runway shortens) · geography (domestic / Asia-Pac / global; blend reduces single-country risk) · Shariah-compliance (pick by family values, performance gap is marginal long-term). Rule: *"Don't optimise for last year's top performer. Optimise for a fund you'll still be holding in 18 years — boring, diversified, and matched to your time horizon."*
3. **Open the account in your child's name.** Minor's name with parent/guardian as trustee; full ownership transfers at 18. Need: child's MyKid/birth cert, parent's MyKad, bank account for auto-debit, licensed UTC.
4. **Set up monthly auto-debit (DCA).** *"Set the debit date a day or two after your salary lands. Treat it like rent — non-negotiable."*
5. **Review annually, not monthly.** Performance vs. benchmark · contribution capacity · risk-profile drift (shift to lower-volatility funds in the final 3–5 years before withdrawal).

### Four canonical "starter mistakes" (reusable as standalone post hooks)
- Waiting for the "right time."
- Starting too aggressively (RM500/mo that stops in 6 months < RM200/mo that runs for 18 years).
- Watching daily fund prices.
- Mixing the fund with other goals (don't dip for renovations / weddings).

### FAQ — objection-handling scripts (Q → A)

**Q: How long will my EPF last in retirement?**
A: It depends on your balance at retirement, your withdrawal rate, and the post-retirement return on your remaining balance. The calculator projects this year by year and shows a depletion age — the age at which EPF runs out if spending rises with inflation. If that age is earlier than your life expectancy, you have a funding gap.

**Q: What is Belanjawanku?**
A: Belanjawanku is the Expenditure Reference Report by EPF/KWSP with the Social Wellbeing Research Centre at Universiti Malaya. The 2024/2025 edition pegs RM2,380/month as the cost of a decent standard of living for a single elderly person — basic needs, physical comfort, and participating in society with dignity. The calculator inflates this figure forward and uses it as a minimum benchmark.

**Q: Why does the calculator assume 4% inflation?**
A: Malaysia's long-run headline inflation has averaged around 2-3%, but real cost-of-living increases for retirees in food, healthcare, and utilities often run higher. A 4% default builds in a margin of safety. It is adjustable between 3% and 6% to test other scenarios.

**Q: What is the difference between nominal and real EPF income?**
A: Nominal income is the literal ringgit amount EPF pays at retirement. Real income, or purchasing power, is that amount in today's money — after stripping out inflation. RM4,000/month in 20 years buys far less than RM4,000 today, so the calculator shows both.

**Q: Why might my EPF run out before I do?**
A: EPF lump-sum withdrawals are designed to fund a finite number of retirement years, not a lifetime annuity. If a target lifestyle grows with inflation while the remaining balance compounds at a lower rate, the maths eventually breaks and the fund depletes before life expectancy. The calculator surfaces this as a depletion-age alert.

**Q: Is this calculator financial advice?**
A: No. It is a projection tool with assumptions you control — actual results depend on your real contribution history, EPF dividend performance, and life events.
