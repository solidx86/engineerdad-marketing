# "Just use EPF i-Invest, same funds cheaper"

> Acknowledges EPF's statutory guaranteed-minimum dividend; frames the i-Invest opportunity cost without ever claiming a unit trust is safer than or beats EPF. Figures dated; EPF terms subject to change by EPF; not personalised advice.

## English

### The myth

"Why pay an agent's sales charge when EPF i-Invest sells the same funds at 0.5%? Same fund, fraction of the cost — just move my EPF money in and skip the middleman."

### The true part

On the entry charge, the myth is simply correct, and it's worth saying so. Buying through **EPF i-Invest self-serve carries a sales charge of just 0.5% per buy transaction**, against up to 5.0% through an agent (corpus/data/datasets/sales-charge-by-channel.json) — for the same families of funds. If the only variable were entry cost, i-Invest is the cheapest legitimate door by a wide margin, and a cost-sensitive EPF member should know it exists. The brand recommends knowing about it, not hiding it.

### The variable it ignores

The ignored variable is **the opportunity cost and the eligibility gates that make i-Invest a constrained option, not a free upgrade** (corpus/data/charts/sales-charge-by-channel.yaml). Three things the "same funds cheaper" framing drops: (1) **money you move into i-Invest stops earning the EPF annual dividend** while it's invested — and EPF carries a **statutory guaranteed-minimum 2.5% dividend under the EPF Act 1991 §27** and has declared well above that recently (5.35–6.30% over 2022–2025; corpus/data/datasets/epf-dividend-history.json). So you're not getting the fund "for free"; you're giving up a partially-guaranteed EPF dividend to take market risk, and the fund has to clear that hurdle before you're ahead. This is an opportunity cost, **not** a claim that the fund is safer than or beats EPF — it may underperform the dividend you gave up. (2) i-Invest is **gated**: members below 55, with investable amount capped at **30% of your Akaun Persaraan savings above your age-band Basic Savings**, minimum RM1,000, recalculated every 3 months — so it can only ever hold a slice of your savings, and many members aren't eligible for much or any. (3) It's self-serve, with no consultant doing suitability or review. The honest turn isn't "use the agent instead"; it's that i-Invest is a cheap channel for *eligible EPF money you're willing to switch from a guaranteed dividend into market risk* — a real but bounded tool, not a costless way to buy the same thing.

### Who it changes the answer for

For an eligible EPF member below 55 with savings comfortably above their Basic Savings, who understands they're trading the EPF dividend for market risk and wants low-cost self-serve access, the myth is right — i-Invest is the cheapest channel and a sensible one. For the saver who hears "same funds cheaper" and assumes it's a pure discount, it changes the answer: you're giving up a §27-floored dividend, you can only move a capped slice, and you may not be eligible at all — the 0.5% is real, but it isn't free.
