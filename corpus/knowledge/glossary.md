# PMB Unit Trust Glossary

> Figures reflect publicly published PMB/LHDN schedules as of 2026-05-31; consultant to confirm current values before use in campaign copy. All term definitions draw from the PMB Master Prospectus definitions section, the FIMM Code of Ethics (4th ed.), and SC investor-education publications. Where industry practice has changed (e.g. bid-offer spread), the change is noted.

## English

**NAV (Net Asset Value)**
: The per-unit value of a fund, calculated as total fund assets minus liabilities divided by units in issue. On a PMB statement, the NAV column shows the price at which holdings were valued on the last valuation date. *Non-obvious clause:* NAV is struck once per business day (typically 5 pm MYT); an instruction submitted after the cut-off uses the *next* business day's NAV — you cannot transact at today's published price if you submit late.

**Unit**
: The fractional ownership interest in a unit trust fund. Buying into a fund purchases a quantity of units at the applicable NAV (plus any sales charge); selling redeems those units at the applicable NAV. Units are recorded in the investor's account, not as physical certificates. On a PMB statement, multiply *units held* by the current NAV to derive current portfolio value.

**Bid-offer spread (retired in PMB's pricing model)**
: Historically, a dual-price model where the "offer price" (buy price) exceeded the "bid price" (sell price), with the gap capturing distribution of sales charges and initial expenses. Public Mutual moved to single-pricing (offer = NAV); charges are now disclosed and collected separately. *Non-obvious clause:* older marketing materials and comparison articles still reference a spread — treat any bid-offer spread claim as outdated unless verified against a current PMB prospectus.

**Sales charge (initial service charge)**
: A percentage fee levied on each new investment, deducted upfront from the investment amount before units are allocated. For PMB equity funds the verified ceiling is **up to 5.0% of NAV per unit**; in practice the charge is computed via the 5/105 method — on a RM1,000 investment: charge = RM1,000 × 5/105 = **RM47.62**, so **RM952.38 enters the fund** as unit value. The PMB Investment Disclaimer states: "A sales charge (if any) will be imposed on purchases of units of the fund and deducted upfront from the investment amount." *Non-obvious clause:* the 5/105 method means the charge is 5% of the net amount that enters the fund (not 5% of the gross amount tendered) — RM952.38 invested × 5% = RM47.62. Compounding never touches the portion lost to the charge.

**Switching fee**
: A charge levied when transferring holdings from one PMB fund to another, typically a percentage of the amount switched or a fixed fee as stated in each fund's PHS (verify current rates against the relevant PHS — figures as of 2026-05-31). *Non-obvious clause:* a switch is a redemption from Fund A followed by a purchase in Fund B — two separate valuation points apply, introducing forward-pricing timing risk on both legs.

**Redemption (withdrawal)**
: The process of selling units back to the fund at the prevailing forward NAV, with proceeds settled to the investor's designated bank account within the fund's stated T+ period. *Non-obvious clause:* for PRS funds, "redemption" before age 55 is legally restricted to permitted reasons only (housing, healthcare, death/disability, or after the first full contribution year at an 8% tax penalty) — the term carries a different operational weight in PRS versus a regular unit trust.

**Distribution (not "dividend")**
: The periodic payout of income from a unit trust fund's realised gains, interest earned, or dividends received from underlying assets. PMB funds use "distribution", not "dividend" — the latter carries corporate-equity connotations that do not apply. *Non-obvious clause:* distribution is not new wealth — on ex-date, the fund's NAV drops by exactly the distributed amount. A RM0.05/unit distribution on a RM1.00 NAV fund leaves the post-ex NAV at RM0.95; total portfolio value is unchanged at the moment of distribution.

**Reinvestment (automatic reinvestment)**
: The option — often the default for PMB accumulation/growth funds — whereby distributions are used to purchase additional units rather than being paid to the investor's bank account. Reinvestment compounds unit holdings without manual action. *Non-obvious clause:* reinvestment units are bought at the post-ex-date NAV (the lower price after distribution is stripped); the total portfolio value at the reinvestment date is therefore unchanged relative to the pre-distribution position — reinvestment does not add value on day zero, it preserves the compounding base.

**Annual management fee (AMF)**
: The ongoing fee charged by the fund manager (Public Mutual Berhad) for managing the fund's investments, expressed as a percentage of the fund's NAV per annum. For PMB equity funds, typically in the region of 1.50% p.a. (verify against each fund's PHS — figures reflect publicly published PMB factsheets as of 2026-05-31; consultant to confirm current values). The AMF is accrued daily and deducted from fund assets, so it is already reflected in the published NAV — investors never receive a separate AMF invoice. *Non-obvious clause:* on an 18-year compounding horizon, a 1.5% p.a. drag materially reduces end-of-horizon value relative to a zero-fee benchmark.

**Trustee fee**
: A separate ongoing charge paid to the independent trustee (statutory custodian of the fund's assets and investor watchdog). For most PMB funds, the trustee is an SC-approved institution such as Maybank Trustees Berhad. Typically in the region of 0.06%–0.08% p.a. (verify against each fund's PHS — consultant to confirm current values). Like the AMF, it is accrued daily, deducted from fund assets, and reflected in the published NAV. *Non-obvious clause:* the trustee is legally empowered to compel the fund manager to remedy breaches of the trust deed — the fee is not just overhead; it pays for an independent compliance layer between the fund manager and investors.

**Ex-date (ex-distribution date)**
: The date on which a fund's NAV is marked down by the declared distribution amount. Investors who hold units before the ex-date receive the distribution (or have it reinvested); investors who buy on or after the ex-date do not receive it. *Non-obvious clause:* buying a fund immediately before the ex-date is a common mistake — the investor pays a NAV that includes the about-to-be-distributed amount, receives the distribution, and may perceive it as a "bonus", but it is a return of part of their own purchase price, not a gain.

**T+ settlement (transaction settlement period)**
: The number of business days after the dealing date by which a transaction is completed — units allocated (for purchases) or proceeds credited (for redemptions). PMB standard settlement is around T+3 business days for most funds (verify against each fund's PHS — consultant to confirm current values). *Non-obvious clause:* "T" is the dealing day on which the instruction is accepted and priced, not the submission day — an instruction received after the cut-off uses the next business day as T, shifting effective settlement later on the calendar.

**Fund Volatility Class (FVC)**
: The standardised risk classification required by SC/FIMM on public references to a fund's category, running from Very Low to Very High on the Lipper Volatility Bands scale. Any public content that references a fund's category must include its FVC. *Non-obvious clause:* FVC measures historical return volatility, not credit risk or liquidity risk — a "Low" FVC fixed-income fund can still lose capital in a rising-rate environment; FVC is emphatically not a capital-guarantee indicator.
