# NAV Cut-off and Settlement: Forward Pricing, T+ and Why Today's Price Isn't Yours

> Figures reflect publicly published PMB dealing procedures as of 2026-05-31; consultant to confirm current cut-off times and settlement periods against the current PHS for each fund before advising a client. Cut-off times and T+ periods can differ by fund. Nothing here constitutes personalised advice.

## English

### Forward pricing — the foundational mechanic

PMB funds use **forward pricing**. This means the price you get when you transact is NOT the NAV published on the day you submit your instruction. Instead, your instruction is priced at the NAV struck *after* your instruction is accepted — at the next valuation point following the fund's cut-off time.

This is by design and is mandated by SC/FIMM regulations to prevent market-timing abuse (investors exploiting known NAV in real time before it is published).

### The cut-off time

PMB's dealing cut-off is **4:00 pm on any business day** (confirmed: publicmutual.com.my investor-guide-investment). Payments received **before or at 4:00 pm** are processed at that same business day's NAV; payments received **after 4:00 pm** are processed at the next business day's price. This applies to over-the-counter investments at Public Bank branches and to electronic channels (PMO / PMO PLUS) alike. (Still verify against the current PHS for any fund-specific exception.) Instructions received:

| Instruction received | Pricing point | Effective dealing day |
|---|---|---|
| Before/at 4:00 pm (e.g. 3:30 pm on Monday) | Monday's NAV (the same business day's valuation) | Monday is "T" |
| After 4:00 pm (e.g. 4:30 pm on Monday) | Tuesday's NAV | Tuesday is "T" |
| On a public holiday or non-business day | Next business day's NAV | Next business day is "T" |

**When the price is actually published is not the same as when it is struck — and it differs by fund type:**

| Fund type | Price published | Valuation it reflects |
|---|---|---|
| Local funds (no foreign investments) | Same business day, **from 8:00 pm** | That same business day's close |
| Funds with foreign investments | Next business day, **from 12:00 pm (noon)** | The *previous* business day's close (after foreign markets settle) |

So the price you see on PMB's website in the **morning** is, for a foreign-invested fund, the **previous business day's** valuation — never a real-time quote. A local fund's same-day price doesn't appear until that evening (8 pm). This is why forward pricing matters: at the 4 pm cut-off you commit before the price you'll get is even published. (Confirmed: publicmutual.com.my investor-guide-investment, fetched 2026-06-01.)

### T+ settlement

"T" is the dealing day (the business day on which the instruction is accepted and priced). Settlement is the number of additional business days before the transaction is complete:

| Transaction type | Typical PMB settlement | Practical meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Purchase (buy) | Within 3 business days from receipt of completed application | Units appear in your account within 3 business days after T; confirmed: publicmutual.com.my investor-guide-investment |
| Redemption (sell) — standard | Within 7 business days from receipt of redemption request | Proceeds credited to bank account within 7 business days; confirmed: publicmutual.com.my investor-guide-redemption |
| Redemption (sell) — e-Series via PMO | Within 4 business days | Expedited; applies to e-Series funds redeemed through PMO (Public Mutual Online) |
| Redemption (sell) — Privilege Circle members | Within 2 business days | Mutual Gold, Elite, Signature, Platinum, Prestige tiers |
| Switch | Settlement applies to each leg separately (verify) | Redemption leg of Fund A and purchase leg of Fund B each settle under their own fund's terms |

*Verify against the current PHS for each fund — settlement periods can differ. The "T+3" framing used in some contexts refers to the purchase leg (3 business days); redemption proceeds follow a longer standard timeline of up to 7 business days.*

### Why this matters in practice

A parent who checks the PMB app on Friday morning, sees a NAV they like, submits a purchase instruction at 5 pm Friday (after cut-off), and gets a public holiday on Monday will find their instruction priced at Tuesday's NAV — a full four calendar days after they decided to act. In volatile market conditions, the gap between the decision NAV and the actual transaction NAV can be material.

### Four things most investors don't know

- **The NAV printed in the morning newspaper or on the PMB portal is yesterday's closing price.** Forward pricing means you can never buy at the last-published NAV; your actual price is set at the next valuation after the cut-off. An investor who says "I bought at RM0.75" is quoting the NAV they saw, not necessarily the NAV they transacted at.
- **Submitting an instruction one minute after the cut-off shifts your pricing by an entire business day.** On a normal weekday that is ~24 hours; on a pre-holiday Friday it could be 3–4 calendar days. The cut-off is a hard line, not a soft guideline.
- **Redemption proceeds take up to 7 business days to reach your bank account under the standard channel.** An investor who redeems to fund an urgent expense on Monday morning should not expect proceeds before the following Wednesday at the earliest under normal conditions — planning liquidity needs without factoring in settlement is a common mistake. (e-Series via PMO: 4 days; Privilege Circle: 2 days.)
- **"T" is the dealing day the instruction is accepted, not the day it is submitted.** A submission after cut-off pushes T to the next business day; the entire settlement chain shifts accordingly. An investor who misses a Friday cut-off has a Monday T, meaning proceeds on standard redemption arrive no sooner than the following Wednesday under normal conditions.
- **Switches involve two separate pricing events.** The redemption leg from Fund A and the purchase leg of Fund B each price at their own fund's next NAV after cut-off. If the two funds have different cut-off times, the effective exchange rate between Fund A and Fund B is only known once both NAVs are struck.

*Framing:* The forward-pricing + settlement mechanic is invisible to most investors until something goes wrong — a redemption that doesn't arrive in time for a tax payment, or a purchase priced at a NAV far from the one that triggered the decision. Making this explicit in onboarding is the kind of technical transparency that analytical parents specifically value.
