### Taxonomy notes

P BOND is the representative **domestic fixed-income (conventional)** entry in the fund family. Key points for a consultant framing this fund:

- Risk level 2 (versus 4–5 for equity funds) reflects materially lower return volatility, not zero risk. Bond funds can and do decline in value, particularly when interest rates rise rapidly.
- The sales charge ceiling for fixed-income funds (up to 1.0%) is materially lower than for equity funds (up to 5.0%) — relevant when positioning this fund as a shorter-horizon or capital-preservation sleeve.
- The annual management fee is also lower (~0.75–1.00% p.a.) versus equity funds (~1.50% p.a.) — but the return expectation is also lower, so the fee-to-return ratio is not automatically better.
- The historical max drawdown of −16.98% (from a 2002 ATH) serves as a reminder that long-duration bond funds carry meaningful interest-rate risk — not a capital-guarantee product.
