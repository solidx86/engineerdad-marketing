§01/ THE PROBLEM

Targeting moved into the creative.

Modern ad platforms have stopped reading audiences and started reading the ad. The lever isn't who you show it to — it's what you show.

The marketing question has changed shape. Audience-stack tuning, interest layering, demographic gates — the levers that defined a decade of paid media — have been quietly subsumed by ranking systems that read each creative's content and match it to the audience themselves. The implication is sharp and operational:

If creative is the targeting lever, you need to produce diverse, compliant, evidence-led creative at volume — and you need to learn from server-side signals, not from the rank charts the platform shows you.

Constraint 1

Volume without sameness

One winning angle cannot be scaled with twenty redraws of the same hook. The engine needs to author distinct hooks, formats, and proof points — across emotional registers — without sliding into slop.

Constraint 2

Server-side signal recovery

Browser pixels under-report. Real conversions live behind cookie loss, app boundaries, and downstream events the pixel never sees. A first-party conversion path is no longer optional.

Constraint 3

Compliance survives volume

Regulated industries can't trade compliance for speed. Every output must be checked at the prompt and again at the write boundary — without slowing the loop or laundering responsibility through automation.

A closed loop, gated by humans at every external write, that learns from server-side signals and keeps getting smarter cycle over cycle. The shape of the response
§02/ THE SHAPE

One cycle, eight stops, six gates.

A single cycle runs end-to-end on demand. Tracking validates the data path. Analytics pulls and ranks signals. The Brain reasons and writes a memo. The production agents downstream — Targeting, Content, Media Production — each terminate in a human gate. Experiment OS designs the test at gate three, then Distribution routes the approved Variants and Articles to their destinations — paused on every platform, awaiting the human flip. After the experiment concludes, the cycle reflects and the next one starts from a smarter base.

Working learning Third confirmation upgrades the Learning to Working. Cross-Pollinate fires — an Authority Article spawned for the winning angle. The platform's frame embedding has settled — new on-frame variants escape learning phase in 10–20 conversions instead of 50.
Steady state Bandit posteriors are sharp. Allocation is heavily exploitative. The 10 bucket holds at structural minimum. CPA stops falling — a slight uptick (4.1 → 4.3) appears as the same audience sees variations of the same frame. Creative decay is becoming visible.
Saturation → pivot Top performer's CPA trend crosses +25 % above its baseline → Sunset fires. Pivot Persona fires concurrently. The 10 bucket grows again (3 / 12) as exploration returns. The new persona's first frame is warm-started by adjacent-embedding transfer — recovery is fast, not cold-start.

Two things to notice. First, the Gate-2 trust ratio (1 / 9 → 12 / 95) widens by an order of magnitude across the first five runs — the human team is approving more scripts to render because the Bandit's picks have earned the call. Second, CPA doesn't fall forever. Saturation surfaces at Run 5 and the system responds with a Pivot in Run 6 rather than burning the winning frame further. The system is designed to survive its own success.

§04/ THE BRAIN INSIDE

Three reasoning paradigms, layered.

The Brain is not a single prompt. It composes a scientific-method outer loop, a Thompson-sampling bandit inner loop, and a self-critique gate. They don't fight each other — each handles a different problem.

OUTER

Scientific Method

Each cycle states predictions with confidence intervals. The next cycle grades them. Confirmed predictions graduate into durable Learnings. Refuted ones are archived with the evidence that killed them. Epistemology.

INNER

Bandit allocation

For each creative arm — a tuple of hook × angle × format × persona × language — maintain a posterior on cost-per-acquisition. Each cycle samples, allocates inverse to sampled CPA, and the familiar 70 / 20 / 10 split emerges from the posterior. Allocation policy.

GATE

Reflexion self-critique

Every cycle's memo opens with a paragraph grading the last one. Where I was right. Where I was overconfident. What I omitted. What I'd do differently. The Self-Critique is the first thing the next cycle reads. Quality check.

Set-piece A — Bandit allocation, emergent.

Start with five arms at uniform allocation. Each cycle samples from each arm's posterior and reallocates budget. Watch the 70 / 20 / 10 buckets surface from the data — they're a consequence of the math, not a fixed input.

§05/ THE SUBSTRATE

Eight agents, seven tool servers, eight review surfaces.

The whole architecture is portable. Agents are markdown files. Tool servers are stdio processes. The review surface is a database under the team's existing knowledge tool. Local SQLite holds insights and creative metadata; a small file indexer holds the grounding corpus. Some tool servers live in other repos — the article-writer MCP runs inside the team's content-site codebase and is called over stdio just like any in-house adapter. Same protocol, different home. No warehouse. No proprietary middleware. Same shape from v1 onwards.

§06/ COMPLIANCE & HUMAN GATES

Three checks, six gates.

Compliance is enforced in three places — once when the agent drafts the work, once when it tries to write to the review surface, and once at the platform boundary where the dangerous parameters simply don't exist. Between those, six human gates ensure that no work crosses an external boundary without a human signing off on it.

Layer A — System-prompt injection
Before drafting

Every content-producing agent loads the compliance block — regulator rules, banned phrases, required disclosures — as the first thing in its working context. The agent reads the rules before it writes a word.

Layer B — Tool-call validation
At the write boundary

The review-surface tool runs a compliance scan on every create call. Banned phrases and missing disclaimers fail the call with a violations list. Fail-closed by design: no row is created. The agent retries; after two failures it surfaces a flag. The Brain's memo is exempt at the row layer (internal artifact) but its synthesis prompt still loads the compliance block.

Layer C — Safe-state hard-wiring
At the platform boundary

Distribution MCPs hard-wire the safe state in the tool schema itself. meta-ads.create_ad always sends status: PAUSED; youtube.upload_video always sends privacyStatus: unlisted; site.draft_article commits but never pushes and never touches the site index. There is no activate_* tool path. The agent cannot pass status: ACTIVE because the parameter doesn't exist. Activation is human-only by construction.

Six human gates

1
Briefs · 12 angles
A human approves which angles get scripts. The bandit's bucket assignment is visible; humans can override before content runs.
2
Scripts + Articles · hooks + body
A human approves which scripts get produced and which articles get delivered. Hook bank is visible; humans can pick or rewrite a different hook before media production.
3
Variants + spec
A human approves the Variant and reviews the per-channel spec fields (paid-platform primary text, video-platform title, article slug) that will ship verbatim. Edit a field directly to override before Distribution runs.
4
Paid platform · flip ACTIVE
Distribution stages ads PAUSED with cell assignments. A human reviews in the ad manager and flips the batch to ACTIVE.
5
Video platform · flip Public
Distribution uploads videos as unlisted. A human reviews on the video platform and toggles to Public when ready.
6
Content site · merge to main
Distribution commits /articles/<slug>.html on the site repo but does not push and does not link it in the site index. A human reviews, links, pushes.
§07/ WHERE THIS GOES — FIGURE iii.

Four marquee bets after v1.2.

v1.2 closes the loop end-to-end through Distribution — humans gate every external write, but the data-entry between gates is automated. v1.5 fills four pieces — first-party conversion recovery, visual signal, generative video, and on-approval triggers — without changing the shape of the system.

BET 01 · v1.5

Production conversion API

An always-on edge function that posts hashed conversion events from the site's own backend — recovering the 30–40 % of conversions browser pixels lose to cookie boundaries. Reuses the same CAPI builder the tracking sandbox already exercises in v1.

BET 02 · v1.5

Visual analytics dashboard

Static-site charts straight off local SQLite. Two pages to start: creative-decay over time per ad, and cost-per-angle windowed. Adds no new data infrastructure — the warehouse stays a single file.

BET 03 · v1.5

Generative video pipeline

A provider abstraction over video aggregators (kie.ai-class) and TTS. Per-bucket cost caps in policy. Optional stock-footage fallback. Static-image rendering is already wired into Media Production via static-renderer + asset-store — this bet is the video half. No changes to the Brain or the review schemas.

BET 04 · v1.5

Webhook automation

Replace the manual /distribute trigger with on-approval webhooks. When a Variant flips to APPROVED at gate three, a webhook fires the same routing the user runs by hand today. The agent prompt and the tool boundary don't change; only the trigger does. Volume-gated — we build this when batch sizes justify it.

And after v1.5 — the same agents and tool servers run unattended on a schedule. The team interacts through the same review surface they already use. Auth moves from local env to team secrets. Triggers move from typed commands to event webhooks. Nothing else changes.

§08/ COLOPHON

What is locked. Eight agents · seven tool servers · eight review surfaces · six human gates · local SQLite · file-indexed corpus · a single workspace · bilingual content artifacts. Three structural principles are also locked: specs-not-artifacts (cross-repo MCP for formats other systems own), write-API safety (safe states hard-wired in the MCP layer; no activate_* tool path), and substance-vs-packaging (content owns substance, media-production owns packaging, distribution owns routing). The shape of the v1 skeleton is the shape of v2 and v3 — scaling depth means filling in agent prompts and adding tools to existing servers, not restructuring.

What is intentionally deferred. Production CAPI deployment, visual analytics, generative video (static-image is already wired), and on-approval webhook triggers all live in v1.5. Each is a thin add — a new tool server or a new deploy target — not a re-architecture. The substrate was designed to absorb them without re-plumbing anything else.

Why this shape. A modern ad platform's algorithm reads creative as targeting; a regulated business can't trade compliance for speed; a human still has to sign off on the work. A closed loop with humans at every external write, compliance enforced at three layers, and a Brain that learns from its own predictions answers all three at once.
