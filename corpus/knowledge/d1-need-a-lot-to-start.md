# "You need a lot of money to start"

> Describes the published minimum-investment mechanics; the budget-and-compounding case is made in the related necessity entries. Figures are dated prospectus minimums; not personalised advice.

## English

### The myth

"Investing in funds is for people with a big lump sum sitting around. I'd need a few thousand ringgit just to get in the door, and I don't have that — so it's not for me yet."

### The true part

The perception has a real source: the standard Public Series entry minimum **is** RM1,000 (corpus/knowledge/a-fee-schedule.md), and a thousand ringgit free-and-clear is a genuine barrier for many families. If the only door a saver has seen is the lump-sum one, "I need a lot to start" is an accurate read of *that* door.

### The variable it ignores

The ignored variable is **the entry mechanics that already exist for small budgets — a RM100 minimum and a monthly commitment instead of a lump sum** (corpus/knowledge/fund-public-regularsavings.md). Public Mutual's e-Series funds drop the entry minimum to **RM100**, and a regular-savings arrangement lets you contribute a fixed amount monthly rather than finding a big sum up front — turning "do I have RM1,000 today" into "can I commit RM100–RM200 a month," which is a different and much smaller question. The lump-sum mental model is the thing doing the gatekeeping, not the product. (The case for *why* a small, regular start is worth making at all — what RM100 a month compounds into, and why "investing is for the rich" is its own myth — is made in corpus/knowledge/d0-cant-afford-to-start.md and corpus/knowledge/d0-investing-is-for-the-rich.md; this entry is only correcting the size-of-entry belief, not re-arguing the compounding.)

### Who it changes the answer for

For a saver who has no monthly surplus at all — whose budget genuinely has no RM100 to spare after essentials — the myth is pointing at a real prior problem, and the honest answer is cash-flow first, not a fund. For the saver who *could* set aside RM100–RM200 a month but is waiting until they have a "proper" lump sum, it changes the answer: the door they're waiting for isn't the only door — the RM100 monthly entry is open now, and the lump-sum requirement they're imagining is a barrier the product already removed.
