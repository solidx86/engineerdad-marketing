# Document Map: Trust Deed, Master Prospectus, PHS, and Factsheet

> Figures reflect the publicly published PMB document structure as of 2026-05-31; consultant to confirm which document governs any specific right or charge before advising a client. Nothing here constitutes legal or personalised financial advice.

## English

### Why the document hierarchy matters

Four documents govern a PMB unit trust investment. Most investors have glanced at one or two; very few know which document controls what. The hierarchy is not arbitrary — each document serves a legally distinct function, and the consequences of misreading which one is binding can be material (e.g. assuming a fee can be waived when the trust deed says otherwise).

### The four documents

| Document | Legal status | Who creates / controls it | What it governs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Trust Deed** | Legally binding contract between the fund manager (Public Mutual Berhad) and the trustee | Executed once at fund launch; amendments require trustee consent and (for material changes) SC approval + investor notice | Fundamental structure: trustee powers, investment mandate limits, permitted asset classes, suspension/deferral of dealings, unit-holder rights |
| **Master Prospectus** | Statutory disclosure document (required by SC) | Updated periodically (typically annually); registered with SC | All fees and charges schedules; switching rights; dealing procedures; risk disclosures; financial reports reference |
| **Product Highlights Sheet (PHS)** | Mandatory SC-prescribed summary for each fund | Issued per fund; updated when material terms change | Fund-specific: sales charge rate, AMF, trustee fee, risk rating (FVC), min investment, settlement period, investment objective |
| **Fund Factsheet** | Non-statutory marketing/information document | Published by PMB typically monthly | Performance data, top holdings, asset allocation, commentary — informational only, not legally binding |

### Which document controls which right

| Question an investor might ask | Governing document |
|---|---|
| "Can the fund suspend redemptions?" | Trust Deed — the suspension clause is in the deed |
| "What sales charge am I paying?" | PHS for the specific fund (and confirmed in the Master Prospectus fees table) |
| "Can I switch to another fund and what does it cost?" | Master Prospectus switching section + target fund's PHS |
| "What assets is the fund allowed to invest in?" | Trust Deed investment mandate + Master Prospectus investment policy |
| "What has the fund returned over the past 3 years?" | Fund Factsheet (informational, not guaranteed) |
| "What is the trustee's role if PMB breaches the investment mandate?" | Trust Deed — trustee enforcement powers |

### What the trustee enforces

The independent trustee (e.g. Maybank Trustees Berhad for many PMB funds) is **not** a passive fee recipient. The trustee:

- Holds legal title to the fund's assets on behalf of unit holders.
- Is empowered by the trust deed to compel the fund manager to remedy breaches of the investment mandate or the deed itself.
- Reports to the SC if the fund manager fails to remedy a breach.
- Reviews all unit holder communications for compliance.

The trustee is the structural reason why investor assets are segregated from Public Mutual Berhad's own corporate assets — PMB could become insolvent without investor assets being attached to its liabilities.

### Four things most investors don't know

- **The fund factsheet is not legally binding.** Performance figures in the factsheet are historical and informational — they carry no contractual weight. The document that binds the fund manager's obligations is the trust deed and master prospectus, not the marketing factsheet.
- **Suspension of redemptions is a trust-deed right, not a manager's discretion.** The conditions under which PMB can lawfully suspend or defer redemptions (e.g. market closure, extraordinary market conditions, force majeure) are defined in the trust deed — the PHS and prospectus summarise them, but the deed is the operative document. An investor who has only read the factsheet has not read the suspension clause.
- **The PHS is fund-specific and can differ materially from fund to fund even within the same PMB family.** A comparison of fees across two PMB equity funds requires reading each fund's PHS separately — the Master Prospectus provides the ceiling rates, but the actual rate for a specific fund may be lower and is only confirmed in its PHS.
- **Material amendments to the trust deed require SC approval and investor notification.** PMB cannot unilaterally change the fundamental investment mandate or investor protections — the trust deed amendment process has statutory gatekeepers. This is a structural investor protection most retail investors do not know exists.

### Practical guidance for a client conversation

When a client asks "where does it say that?" the answer depends on what "that" is:
- Fee amounts → PHS (binding) + Master Prospectus (reference)
- Past performance → Factsheet (informational only)
- Redemption rights and restrictions → Master Prospectus + Trust Deed
- Trustee powers → Trust Deed

Directing clients to the right document prevents a class of misunderstanding where a client believes a claim in the factsheet carries the same weight as a clause in the trust deed.

*Framing:* The document hierarchy is the literacy baseline for any investor who wants to understand what they actually signed up for — not what the brochure says. An analytical parent who knows which document is binding and which is informational has a fundamentally different relationship with their investment than one who only reads the factsheet.
