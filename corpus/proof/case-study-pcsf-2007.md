# PCSF 2007 — A Public Record Case Study in FOMO

> Public Mutual's launch of the Public China Select Fund (PCSF) in 2007 is one of the most-cited cautionary tales in the EngineerDad archive. Use as anchor proof for any post on FOMO, market timing, or the case for DCA over lump-sum-at-peak.

## English

### The story
- 2007: Shanghai Composite surging; China-bull narrative dominant globally.
- Public Mutual launches the **Public China Select Fund (PCSF)** at **RM0.25** per unit.
- FOMO at all-time highs. Investors crowded in.
- The fund hits **RM1 billion** in net asset value in just **8 weeks** of distribution.
- By **March 2008**, the global financial crisis sets in. All of the launch-period gains are *completely wiped out*.

### What this case proves

**It proves the FOMO bug, not the bug in the fund.** PCSF was a competently-managed fund — the lesson is not "Public Mutual launched a bad fund." The lesson is that *investor behaviour at launch* (lump-sum at euphoria, panic-sell at trough) is where capital was lost.

**It proves the DCA case.** Investors who *kept buying* PCSF month-after-month through the crash, at lower and lower unit prices, eventually recovered as the fund recovered. Investors who lump-summed at launch and sold in panic at the bottom locked in the permanent loss.

**It proves "volatility is a feature, not a bug."** A 50%+ drawdown is the price of long-horizon equity exposure. The investors who treated it as the price paid kept their capital. The investors who treated it as the system failing crystallised the loss.

### How to use this case in content

Lead-in pattern:
> "Remember 2007? Shanghai surging. Everyone wanted in on China. Public Mutual launched PCSF at 25 sen, and in 8 weeks it pulled in a billion ringgit. By March 2008 — gone. All of it…"

Then diagnose:
- The bug: lump-sum at peak euphoria, panic-sell at trough.
- The patch: DCA, which would have *bought more units cheaper* through the crash.
- The principle: market timing is the bug. Time-in-market with DCA is the patch.

### Sourcing

Public Mutual annual reports + press archive cover the launch and the post-2008 history. For any creative referencing this case, cite as: *"Public Mutual historical fund record (PCSF, 2007–2008)."* Do not project recovery numbers without sourcing them to the fund's own factsheet history.

## Bahasa Malaysia

### Cerita
- 2007: Shanghai Composite melonjak; naratif bull-China mendominasi global.
- Public Mutual melancarkan **Public China Select Fund (PCSF)** pada **RM0.25** seunit.
- FOMO pada paras tertinggi. Pelabur berhimpun.
- Dana mencapai **RM1 bilion** dalam nilai aset bersih hanya dalam **8 minggu**.
- Menjelang **Mac 2008**, krisis kewangan global menyerang. Semua keuntungan tempoh pelancaran *terhapus sepenuhnya*.

### Apa yang dibuktikan kes ini
- Membuktikan **bug FOMO**, bukan bug dalam dana itu sendiri.
- Membuktikan kes untuk **DCA**: pelabur yang *terus membeli* PCSF setiap bulan melalui krash akhirnya pulih bersama dana.
- Membuktikan **"kemeruapan ialah ciri, bukan bug"**.

### Bagaimana menggunakan kes ini dalam kandungan

Pola pembukaan:
> "Ingat 2007? Shanghai melonjak. Semua orang nak masuk China. Public Mutual lancar PCSF pada 25 sen, dan dalam 8 minggu ia tarik RM1 bilion. Menjelang Mac 2008 — hilang. Semuanya…"

Kemudian diagnos:
- Bug: lump-sum pada kemuncak euforia, jual panik pada lembah.
- Tampalan: DCA, yang akan *membeli lebih banyak unit lebih murah* melalui krash.
