# Monthly Fund Review (MFR) Framework

> EngineerDad's proprietary methodology for evaluating Public Mutual's retail unit-trust funds. Differentiates the brand from "top 5 funds for 2026" listicle finfluencer content. Reuse it as the anchor for any monthly fund-review content (carousel + reel + YouTube long-form).
> Source: IG carousel "Engineer's Approach to Analyze Public Mutual Monthly Funds Review (MFR)" + Monthly Fund Review YouTube playlist.

## English

### What MFR is
A monthly evaluation of all 171 Public Mutual retail unit-trust funds, run with a consistent rubric the audience can verify and reproduce. The output is *not* "top 5 funds to buy this month" — that framing is explicitly avoided. The output is a structured read of which funds are doing their job (beating their benchmark on a risk-adjusted basis), which are drifting, and where data anomalies are worth a deeper look.

### What MFR measures (and what each metric is named in-brand)

**Alpha** — return *above benchmark*. The brand framing: *"the true engine power of a fund manager."* If a fund returns 8% but its benchmark returned 9%, that fund has *negative* alpha — it failed at its job, even though the headline number looks fine. Alpha is the metric that separates "the market did the work" from "the manager did the work."

**Alpha Efficiency** — alpha adjusted for the volatility taken to earn it. Brand framing: *"high returns without the heart-stopping volatility."* A fund that earns 10% alpha through wild swings is doing a different job than a fund earning 7% alpha smoothly. Long-horizon parents typically want the smooth one.

**Top 3 by Category** — the leaderboard split four ways: Equity, Mixed Asset, Fixed Income, Money Market. Each category has different benchmarks and different volatility regimes; comparing across categories is apples to oranges.

**The Smartest Engines** — best risk-adjusted returns across the whole sample. The brand's name for funds that combine high alpha *and* high alpha efficiency. These are the candidates worth a closer read.

**Reverse-engineered anomalies** — when a fund's headline number looks too good to be true, treat it as a debugging task: what *combination* of holdings, allocation moves, or one-off events produced this? Example: PIATAF (Public Islamic Asia Tactical Allocation Fund) delivered a 30.11% 3-year annualised return — the brand walks audiences through the reverse-engineering rather than just amplifying the number.

### What MFR does *not* do
- It does not declare a "buy this fund" pick. SC + FIMM rules forbid public fund-specific recommendations; the brand respects this on every public asset.
- It does not cherry-pick favourable timeframes. If a fund passes on 1-year but fails on 5-year, the report says so.
- It does not compare Public Mutual funds head-to-head with named competitor funds (FIMM rule).
- It does not project forward returns. Past alpha does not predict future alpha.

### The headline result format

Every MFR opens with a single-line summary that the rest of the post unpacks. February 2026 example:

> "Out of 171 Public Mutual retail funds, only 65% (111 funds) consistently beat their benchmark across multiple time frames in February 2026."

That number is the hook. The rest of the post diagnoses *why* — sector drift, currency moves, allocation calls — and then walks through the Top 3 by Category and the Smartest Engines.

### How to write an MFR post (recipe)
1. **Headline data point** (e.g., "65% passed").
2. **Define the rubric** — alpha, alpha efficiency, the four categories. Plain English the first time, every month.
3. **Top 3 by Category** — leaderboard, no recommendation.
4. **The Smartest Engines** — risk-adjusted standouts.
5. **The anomaly of the month** — one fund whose number is unusual; reverse-engineer it. This is where engineering voice lives.
6. **The closing principle** — one of the brand's standing rules ("Don't optimise for last year's top performer"; "Volatility is a feature, not a bug").
7. **FIMM compliance block** — verbatim, every time.

### Why this differentiates
Most finfluencer fund-review content is a leaderboard with no methodology. MFR is a methodology with a leaderboard as a byproduct. That difference is the brand's moat — and the reason MFR content travels well from IG to YouTube long-form to investor conversations.

## Bahasa Malaysia

### Apa itu MFR
Penilaian bulanan semua 171 dana amanah saham runcit Public Mutual, dijalankan dengan rubrik konsisten yang boleh disahkan dan dihasilkan semula oleh audiens. Output bukan "5 dana terbaik untuk dibeli bulan ini" — bingkai itu sengaja dielakkan. Output adalah bacaan berstruktur tentang dana mana yang melaksanakan tugasnya, mana yang menyimpang, dan di mana anomali data berbaloi untuk diteliti.

### Apa yang MFR ukur

**Alpha** — pulangan *melebihi benchmark*. Bingkai jenama: *"kuasa enjin sebenar pengurus dana."* Jika dana memulangkan 8% tetapi benchmark-nya memulangkan 9%, dana itu mempunyai alpha *negatif* — ia gagal pada tugasnya, walaupun nombor utama kelihatan baik.

**Alpha Efficiency** — alpha yang dilaraskan untuk volatiliti yang diambil untuk mendapatkannya. Bingkai jenama: *"pulangan tinggi tanpa kemeruapan jantung berhenti."*

**3 Teratas mengikut Kategori** — leaderboard dibahagikan empat cara: Ekuiti, Aset Campuran, Pendapatan Tetap, Pasaran Wang.

**Smartest Engines** — pulangan dilaraskan risiko terbaik dalam keseluruhan sampel.

**Anomali yang direkayasa balik** — apabila nombor utama dana kelihatan terlalu baik untuk benar, layannya sebagai tugas *debugging*: apa kombinasi pegangan, gerakan peruntukan, atau peristiwa sekali sahaja menghasilkan ini?

### Apa yang MFR *tidak* lakukan
- Tidak mengisytiharkan pilihan "beli dana ini". Peraturan SC + FIMM melarang cadangan dana spesifik secara awam.
- Tidak memilih jangka masa yang menguntungkan sahaja.
- Tidak membandingkan dana Public Mutual berhadap-hadapan dengan dana pesaing yang dinamakan.
- Tidak mengunjurkan pulangan masa depan. Alpha lalu tidak meramal alpha masa depan.

### Format keputusan utama

Setiap MFR dibuka dengan ringkasan satu baris. Contoh Februari 2026:

> "Daripada 171 dana runcit Public Mutual, hanya 65% (111 dana) secara konsisten mengalahkan benchmark mereka merentas pelbagai jangka masa pada Februari 2026."

### Resipi menulis pos MFR
1. Titik data utama
2. Tentukan rubrik
3. 3 Teratas mengikut Kategori
4. Smartest Engines
5. Anomali bulan ini
6. Prinsip penutup
7. Blok pematuhan FIMM
