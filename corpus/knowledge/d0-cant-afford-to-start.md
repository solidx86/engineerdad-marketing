# "I don't earn enough to invest"

> Describes the mechanics and trade-offs of small, regular contributions; all RM figures are illustrative model output at a stated assumption, dated, and not a forecast or guaranteed outcome. Nothing here is personalised advice — read the relevant Master Prospectus and Product Highlights Sheet (PHS) before investing.

## English

### The myth

"Investing is something you do once you have spare money. My income barely covers the month — there's nothing left over to put in. I'll start when I earn more."

### The true part

This is genuinely correct on its own terms: you cannot invest money you do not have, and forcing contributions on top of an already-stretched budget is bad advice. If the monthly numbers truly do not close, the first job is cash-flow and emergency-buffer work, not a fund account. Telling someone with no slack to "just invest" ignores their real constraint. The concession is real — the objection only fails on the size of the entry it assumes.

### The variable it ignores

The myth quietly assumes the entry ticket is large. It is not. Through the online e-Series channel, the minimum initial **and** additional investment is **RM100** — a tenth of the RM1,000 standard Public Series minimum (corpus/knowledge/a-fee-schedule.md, sourced from the e-Series Master Prospectus p. 60). The question is therefore not "do I earn enough" but "can I redirect RM100 a month."

What that small amount becomes is the part the myth never reaches. At an 8% illustrative annual return (corpus/data/datasets/compounding-table.json):

| Monthly amount | After 20 years | After 30 years |
|---|---|---|
| RM100 | ~RM58,900 | ~RM149,000 |
| RM200 | ~RM117,800 | ~RM298,100 |

And the money is often already there, leaking. Redirecting a RM240/month coffee habit at the same 8% over 18 years projects to roughly **RM115,000** (corpus/data/charts/coffee-to-compound.yaml) — more than a local degree. The constraint most people describe as "not enough income" is, on inspection, "not yet redirected spending." Figures are illustrative; actual fund returns vary year to year and past performance is not indicative of future results.

### Who it changes the answer for

For the household whose budget genuinely does not close — net negative after essentials — the myth is not a myth; the honest first step is cash flow, debt, and an emergency buffer, and a Consultant who pushes a fund there is doing it wrong. For the much larger group who *do* have RM100/month somewhere in discretionary spend but believe the door only opens at RM1,000 or at "a higher salary," the answer flips: the barrier was the assumed entry size, not the income.
