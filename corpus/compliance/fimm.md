# FIMM — Code of Ethics & Marketing Rules for Registered Persons

> Compliance grounding for AI marketing agents acting on behalf of a Public Mutual Unit Trust Consultant (UTC) + PRS Consultant. Authority: Federation of Investment Managers Malaysia (FIMM), the SC-recognised Self-Regulatory Organisation (SRO) for the unit trust and PRS industry. Primary instrument: **FIMM Code of Ethics, Fourth Edition, 10 October 2023** (the "FIMM Code"), issued under FIMM Consolidated Rules ¶2.2.2(a). Supplementary: FIMM's Guidance Notes on the SC Advertising Guidelines (Revised 26 September 2025), and FIMM Consolidated Rules.

## English

### Governing references

- **FIMM Code of Ethics, Fourth Edition** — effective 10 October 2023, supersedes the Third Edition (13 January 2020). Five Core Principles + Chapter 4 list of prohibited conduct + Chapter 5 non-compliance regime.
- **FIMM Consolidated Rules** — issued by FIMM as a SRO. The Code is mandated under ¶2.2.2(a); the Guidance Notes under ¶2.2.2(y).
- **FIMM Guidance Notes on the SC Advertising Guidelines** (Rev. 26 Sept 2025) — UTS/PRS interpretations of SC-GL/ADV-2020 (R1-2025). See `sc-malaysia.md` for the SC-side rules; this document captures FIMM's adoption + supervisor obligations.
- **Capital Markets and Services Act 2007 (CMSA)** — sections 76 (registered persons) and 377 (SC's rule-making power) anchor FIMM's authority.
- **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)** — strict client confidentiality; FIMM enforces compliance via Core Principle 5 + Chapter 4 §4.7.

### Who is bound (FIMM Code §1.2, §2.1)

The Code binds:
- **Distributors** — corporations registered and authorised by FIMM to market and distribute Schemes (Public Mutual Berhad as the principal Distributor for our consultant).
- **Consultants** — individuals registered with FIMM and authorised to market and distribute Schemes (the licensed UTC + PRS Consultant on this account).
- **Officers and Personnel** of a Distributor — directors, secretaries, employees, and appointed receivers.

A Consultant's registration is "tied to" a Principal (the Distributor); a Consultant may only represent **one Principal** for UTS or PRS at any one time (¶3.22), and may only market/distribute Schemes that the Distributor itself distributes (¶3.23).

### Scope (FIMM Code §1.2, §1.3)

The Code applies in two contexts:
1. **Marketing and distribution of unit trust schemes (UTS) and private retirement schemes (PRS).**
2. **Any activity that may adversely impact the Industry.** (Wide tail-clause — covers public-channel conduct that doesn't directly market a Scheme but reflects on the consultant's licensed status.)

> **"Marketing and distribution" defined (§2.1):** "promotion, offering, marketing and distribution of Schemes in any form, including engaging or attempting to engage in any act to promote, offer, market and distribute Schemes to any person."

This sweeping definition catches every public-channel content piece that names, references, or implies promotion of a Public Mutual fund or PRS scheme — including reposts, livestreams, podcasts, AEO articles, paid ads, and DMs.

### Five Core Principles (FIMM Code Chapter 3)

#### Core Principle 1: Honesty and Integrity (¶3.1–§3.4)

- ¶3.1 — "A Distributor and Consultant shall at all times act honestly and with integrity."
- ¶3.2 — "A Distributor and Consultant shall at all times act in the best interest of investors."
- ¶3.3 — "A Distributor and Consultant shall deal with investors in good faith and ensure fair treatment of investors."
- ¶3.4 — "A Distributor and Consultant shall uphold and protect the reputation of the Industry."

#### Core Principle 2: Professionalism (¶3.5–§3.24)

- ¶3.5 — "demonstrate high standards of professionalism and shall exercise due care, skill and diligence."
- ¶3.6 — "provide prompt, efficient and continuous service to investors, including treating investors with respect."
- ¶3.7 — **Mandatory suitability assessment** — "shall conduct suitability assessment in accordance with the requirements provided under relevant securities laws and guidelines issued by the SC and FIMM **prior to recommending any Schemes to an investor**." (i.e., the Suitability Assessment Form / SAF process — public-channel content cannot substitute.)
- ¶3.9–§3.10 — incentives and gifts must not distract investors from informed decisions.
- ¶3.11 — AML/CFT compliance (SC's Guidelines on Prevention of Money Laundering and Terrorism Financing).
- ¶3.12 — open and co-operative dealing with FIMM and other regulators.
- ¶3.19 — **Distributor's supervisory duty:** "A Distributor shall monitor compliance of their Consultants on the requirements of this Code, FIMM Rules, the securities laws, guidelines and other relevant laws, including notifying, explaining and conducting the necessary training to their Consultants." (This is the Public Mutual / UTC chain of accountability — Public Mutual is on the hook for our consultant's marketing.)
- ¶3.22–§3.23 — single-Principal rule + can only distribute Schemes of that Principal.
- ¶3.24 — "A Consultant must show proof of his registration FIMM to investors in the manner specified by FIMM when carrying out the marketing and distribution of Schemes." (i.e., FIMM registration number must be visible on profile and content.)

#### Core Principle 3: Avoidance of Conflict of Interest (¶3.25–§3.27)

- ¶3.25 — avoid conflicts when dealing with investors.
- ¶3.26 — where unavoidable, disclose in writing to the investor and prioritise investor's interest.
- ¶3.27 — Distributor must have systems/policies/procedures to identify, avoid, disclose, manage conflicts.

#### Core Principle 4: Disclosure of Information (¶3.28–§3.30)

- ¶3.28 — provide accurate, timely, adequate information about Schemes (prospectus, disclosure document, PHS).
- ¶3.29 — clear explanation of Scheme features, including:
  (a) investment objectives;
  (b) investment strategy;
  (c) calculation method on unit prices;
  (d) **risks of investing in the Schemes, including the risk of investing through loan financing**;
  (e) unique features;
  (f) distribution policy;
  (g) fees, charges, expenses + calculation details;
  (h) restrictions, including early-withdrawal fees;
  (i) tax implications;
  (j) cooling-off period.

#### Core Principle 5: Observance of Confidentiality (¶3.31–§3.34)

- ¶3.31 — "shall safeguard investors' personal and financial information at all times, except where disclosure has been authorised in writing by the investor or where such disclosure is required to be made to any relevant authority pursuant to any relevant laws or legal process."
- ¶3.32 — Distributor must have systems/policies/procedures to safeguard investor information.
- ¶3.33 — Officers shall not make improper use of investor information for personal gain or to harm investors.
- ¶3.34 — only the servicing Consultant or an authorised Officer may access an investor's information.

### Chapter 4 — Prohibited conduct (full list)

This chapter is the operative ban-list. Each item is verbatim or near-verbatim. **Any of these committed via marketing content is a misconduct.**

#### Under Core Principle 1 (¶4.1)

A Distributor and Consultant shall **not**:

- **(a)** misappropriate investor funds.
- **(b)** knowingly or recklessly falsify documents or submit falsified documents.
- **(c)** commit forgery, including forging signatures.
- **(d)** amend or alter any information provided by an investor without his written consent.
- **(e)** use any document or information where the content is false or misleading.
- **(f)** **misrepresent or make false or exaggerated statements concerning the Schemes**, including:
  - (i) characteristics of the Schemes;
  - (ii) past or present performances of the Schemes;
  - (iii) **using names or advertisement expressions that are likely to mislead an investor into investing in the Schemes**;
  - (iv) using outdated facts or information when dealing with an investor;
  - (v) **disclosing only favourable features of the Schemes**;
  - (vi) **comparing Schemes using unrelated characteristics**.
- **(g)** make false statement or disseminate false information concerning the Schemes, SC, FIMM, the Industry, other Distributor or Consultant.
- **(h)** **use or display the title 'financial planner', 'financial advisor', 'investment advisor' or 'investment consultant' unless authorised** by the relevant regulatory authorities or professional bodies.

¶4.2 — A Distributor and Consultant shall **not** deal in:
- **(a)** Schemes or other investment products not authorised or recognised under the securities laws.
- **(b)** Schemes or products they are not licensed/registered to market and distribute.
- **(c)** Schemes or products on any **alert list** by the relevant authorities.

> **Practical application:** ¶4.1(f)(iii) catches "best-performing", "miracle fund" naming/expressions. ¶4.1(f)(v) catches single-window cherry-picking. ¶4.1(f)(vi) catches comparing a Public Mutual equity fund to an EPF blended-portfolio dividend (unrelated characteristics). ¶4.2 catches any reference, repost, or recommendation of forex/crypto/individual-stock-pick services.

#### Under Core Principle 2 (¶4.3)

A Distributor and Consultant shall **not**:

- **(a)** use deception, misleading, unfair or aggressive method to recruit prospective Consultants.
- **(b)** act on behalf of an investor in submitting a transaction without a valid instrument.
- **(c)** accept cash or cash deposits from investors.
- **(d)** **engage in aggressive and offensive sales practices.**
- **(e)** allow any investor to transfer money or issue a cheque to any account other than the Distributor's account.
- **(f)** request or accept pre-signed/pre-thumbprint forms.
- **(g)** appoint or allow a non-registered person to market or distribute Schemes.
- **(h)** **provide statements or information which create confusion or misunderstanding.**
- **(i)** provide cash discount or rebate to an investor.
- **(j)** perform churning (excessive redemption + repurchase).
- **(k)** **use advertisement and promotional materials which do not comply with the SC's Guidelines on Advertising for Capital Market Products and Related Services.**
- **(l)** make negative or unwarranted statement that adversely affects the interest of an investor or the reputation of the Industry, FIMM, or the SC.
- **(m)** **encourage investors to invest in any Scheme through loan financing.**
- **(n)** **use FIMM's logo without FIMM's prior written consent**, except to disclose membership/registration with FIMM.

¶4.4 — A **Consultant** shall **not**:
- **(a)** impersonate or use the identity of another Consultant.
- **(b)** assign or allocate sales of any Schemes to another Consultant.
- **(c)** **use advertisement and promotional materials which are not provided or approved by his Distributor.**
- **(d)** use any valuation methodology other than those formally adopted by his Distributor.
- **(e)** make an investment decision on behalf of the investor.
- **(f)** charge or receive fees other than those disclosed in the prospectus / disclosure document / PHS.

> **¶4.4(c) is the sharpest marketing rule for our consultant.** Every promotional asset (creative, script, article, hook bank, AEO authority article) requires Distributor approval before it can be classified as "Public Mutual / UTC marketing." The agentic system MUST treat Distributor sign-off as a hard gate. Without it, the Consultant is using "advertisement and promotional materials which are not provided or approved by his Distributor" — a Chapter 4 misconduct.

#### Under Core Principle 3 (¶4.5)

- **¶4.5** — shall not perform any switching transaction for an investor to generate sales commission or gain other benefits, where it is not in the best interest of investors.

#### Under Core Principle 4 (¶4.6)

A Distributor and Consultant shall **not**:

- **(a)** **provide forecast of future performance of any Scheme.**
- **(b)** **make statement on yields or returns of any Scheme without any objective and reasonable basis.**
- **(c)** **conceal the different characteristics of each Scheme when making comparisons with other Schemes.**
- **(d)** provide information on matters in which they are not competent to deal in.

> **¶4.6(a) is absolute on Scheme-specific forecasts.** Read with SC §8.14 (forward-looking statements with reasonable basis): asset-class / market forecasts can be permissible; **specific-fund forecasts cannot**. Agents that draft creative or scripts must avoid "Fund X is expected to deliver Y%" framings entirely.

#### Under Core Principle 5 (¶4.7)

A Distributor and Consultant shall **not**:
- disclose investor's personal and financial information to a third party without prior written consent or unless required by law.
- acquire or use information about an investor without proper authorisation.
- disseminate or exchange investor information with another Distributor, Consultant, or third party.
- request or procure investor information from a third party.

> **Practical application of ¶4.7:** WhatsApp screenshots, account snapshots, application forms, portfolio screenshots — **no public posting whatsoever**, even with names/numbers redacted. The very act of soliciting or disseminating client material publicly without explicit written authorisation breaches Chapter 5.

### Mandatory profile / identity disclosure

Drawn from FIMM Code ¶3.24 + ¶4.1(h) + the FIMM Guidance Notes' general application:

1. **FIMM registration number** must appear in all consultant-led marketing channels — bio, channel description, profile of every active platform.
2. **Title must be exactly "Public Mutual Unit Trust Consultant"** (and "PRS Consultant" if applicable). Visible in bio.
3. **Banned titles** (¶4.1(h)): "Financial Planner", "Financial Advisor", "Investment Advisor", "Investment Consultant" — unless the consultant separately holds a CMSRL.
4. **Distributor name** (Public Mutual Berhad) should appear in the consultant's bio so the Principal–Consultant relationship is visible.
5. **FIMM logo usage** is permitted only to disclose registration/membership (¶4.3(n)); any other usage requires FIMM's prior written consent.

### Marketing & promotional rules under FIMM (cross-references)

The FIMM Guidance Notes (26 Sept 2025) interpret the SC Advertising Guidelines for the UTS/PRS industry. The substantive rules on phrasing, target audience, performance data, comparisons, disclaimers, channel choice, finfluencers, and testimonials are captured in `sc-malaysia.md`. FIMM's own additions:

- **Banned-statement examples (FIMM GN Table A)** — exhaustive UTS/PRS list of "do not say this" statements covering exaggeration, risk-downplay, get-rich-quick, and FOMO. See `sc-malaysia.md` §"Prohibited content — Red Flag Phrasing".
- **Testimonial controls for celebrities/influencers (¶3.1–§3.5)** — higher-rank approval, signed compliance declaration, optional minimum exclusivity period.
- **High-Risk product disclaimer recommendation (¶4.5)** — at minimum, Distributors must include a target-audience disclaimer for High-Risk products. Encouraged for all products.
- **Channel-fitness table (Table C)** — Corporate Website / Online Ads / Print Media / TV-Radio / Social Media (Corporate Accounts Only) / Email Marketing — each with compliance tips.
- **Internet legibility rule (¶7.7)** — fonts ≥ 10-point Times New Roman or visually equivalent; sufficient colour contrast.

### Suitability & one-on-one rule

- **¶3.7 (mandatory):** suitability assessment **prior** to any Scheme recommendation.
- **Public-channel posts must not function as a sales pipeline shortcut** around the SAF process. Any creative that names a specific fund and prompts a buy decision violates ¶3.7 + SC §8.01.
- Specific fund recommendations belong in **private one-on-one consultations** following a documented SAF.

### Record-keeping & supervisor sign-off

- ¶3.16 — Distributor must have systems to identify/verify/document every transaction and maintain integrity of investor information.
- ¶3.17 — Distributor must have a complaints regime: fair handling, recording, sufficient resources, investor notification of SIDREC recourse, quarterly reports to FIMM/SC, prompt notification of any complaint that may adversely affect investor interest or industry reputation.
- ¶3.19 — Distributor must monitor Consultant compliance with the Code/FIMM Rules/securities laws, including training.
- ¶4.4(c) — Consultant cannot use any promotional material **not provided or approved by the Distributor**. Practical implication: every piece of consultant-led creative must pass through Public Mutual's approval workflow before it is treated as "approved promotional material."

### Disciplinary regime (FIMM Code Chapter 5)

- ¶5.1 — non-compliance with the Code or FIMM Rules constitutes **misconduct**.
- ¶5.2 — upon discovery, the Distributor must establish a prima facie case and submit an investigation report to FIMM.
- ¶5.3 — attempts, preparatory acts, and abetting all count as misconduct.
- ¶5.4 — abetting includes instigating, conspiring, or intentionally aiding (by act or omission).
- ¶5.5 — sanctions per **Chapter 6 of FIMM Consolidated Rules** — public reprimand, fines, suspension, deregistration, referral to SC for enforcement action.
- ¶5.6 — **whistleblower duty**: any Personnel or Consultant of a Distributor who has reason to believe the Distributor has failed to comply must immediately report to FIMM.
- ¶5.7 — whistleblower identity is kept confidential to the extent permissible by law.

### Cross-references to SC

FIMM does not replace SC rules — it implements and supplements them at the industry level:
- **FIMM CoE ¶4.3(k)** explicitly mandates compliance with **SC's Guidelines on Advertising for Capital Market Products and Related Services** (i.e., everything in `sc-malaysia.md`).
- **FIMM CoE ¶3.7 + ¶3.11** anchor the SAF process to SC and FIMM securities-law requirements.
- **FIMM CoE ¶3.12** mandates open dealing with FIMM **and** the SC.
- **FIMM Guidance Notes** are explicitly an interpretation aid for SC-GL/ADV-2020 (R1-2025); inconsistencies resolve in favour of the SC's rules (FIMM GN ¶1.6).

### Open questions / ambiguities

- **"Provided or approved" by the Distributor (¶4.4(c))** — the Code does not specify the form of Distributor approval. Until Public Mutual confirms a specific approval workflow, the safe assumption is that **any** consultant-led creative requires sign-off, and the agentic system should add an explicit "Distributor pre-approval evidence" field to every CreativeVariant before it can be ranked compliant.
- **Reposting Distributor / Public Mutual corporate content** — likely permissible without separate sign-off (it's already "provided" by the Distributor) **provided** the consultant adds no commentary that could re-frame the message.
- **"Activity that may adversely impact the Industry" (¶1.2(b))** — wide tail-clause. Practically: avoid public commentary on competitors' funds, on FIMM/SC actions, or on Public Mutual that could reasonably be read as harming industry reputation.

### Skop

Kod ini terpakai kepada:
- **Pengedar (Distributors)** — Public Mutual Berhad bagi perunding ini.
- **Perunding (Consultants)** — individu berdaftar FIMM.
- **Pegawai dan Personnel** Pengedar.

Perunding hanya boleh mewakili **satu Principal** untuk UTS atau PRS pada satu masa (¶3.22) dan hanya boleh mengedar Skim Pengedar tersebut (¶3.23).

### Definisi "pemasaran dan pengedaran" (§2.1)

> "Promosi, penawaran, pemasaran dan pengedaran Skim dalam apa-apa bentuk, termasuk melibatkan diri atau cuba melibatkan diri dalam apa-apa perbuatan untuk mempromosi, menawarkan, memasarkan dan mengedar Skim kepada mana-mana orang."

Definisi ini meliputi setiap kandungan saluran awam yang merujuk dana Public Mutual atau skim PRS — termasuk perkongsian semula, siaran langsung, podcast, artikel kepakaran, iklan berbayar, DM.

### Lima Prinsip Teras

#### Prinsip Teras 1: Kejujuran dan Integriti (¶3.1–§3.4)

- Bertindak jujur, dengan integriti, dalam kepentingan terbaik pelabur, dengan niat baik, melindungi reputasi industri.

#### Prinsip Teras 2: Profesionalisme (¶3.5–§3.24)

- Standard profesional tinggi, kemahiran wajar.
- **Penilaian kesesuaian wajib (¶3.7)** sebelum mengesyorkan Skim.
- AML/CFT (¶3.11).
- Kerjasama terbuka dengan FIMM dan SC.
- **Tugas penyeliaan Pengedar (¶3.19):** memantau pematuhan Perunding.
- **Satu Principal** sahaja (¶3.22) + hanya Skim Principal tersebut (¶3.23).
- Tunjuk bukti pendaftaran FIMM kepada pelabur (¶3.24) — nombor pendaftaran FIMM mesti kelihatan dalam profil dan kandungan.

#### Prinsip Teras 3: Mengelak Konflik Kepentingan (¶3.25–§3.27)

- Elakkan; jika tidak boleh dielakkan, dedahkan secara bertulis dan utamakan kepentingan pelabur.

#### Prinsip Teras 4: Pendedahan Maklumat (¶3.28–§3.30)

- Maklumat tepat / tepat masa / mencukupi: prospektus, dokumen pendedahan, PHS.
- Penjelasan jelas: objektif pelaburan, strategi, harga unit, **risiko (termasuk pembiayaan pinjaman)**, ciri unik, dasar agihan, yuran, sekatan, implikasi cukai, tempoh penyejukan.

#### Prinsip Teras 5: Kerahsiaan (¶3.31–§3.34)

- Lindungi maklumat peribadi dan kewangan pelabur kecuali kebenaran bertulis atau diperlukan undang-undang.

### Bab 4 — Tingkah laku terlarang

#### Di bawah Prinsip 1 (¶4.1)

Pengedar dan Perunding **tidak** boleh:
- Salahguna dana pelabur.
- Memalsu dokumen / mengemukakan dokumen palsu.
- Pemalsuan tandatangan.
- Mengubah maklumat pelabur tanpa kebenaran bertulis.
- Mengguna dokumen / maklumat palsu atau mengelirukan.
- **Salah gambarkan / kenyataan palsu / berlebihan tentang Skim** (ciri / prestasi lalu / nama / ungkapan iklan mengelirukan / fakta lapuk / hanya ciri positif / perbandingan ciri tidak berkaitan).
- Tersebarkan maklumat palsu tentang Skim, SC, FIMM, industri, atau Pengedar/Perunding lain.
- **Menggunakan tajuk 'Financial Planner', 'Financial Advisor', 'Investment Advisor', 'Investment Consultant' tanpa kebenaran kawal selia berkenaan.**

¶4.2 — tidak boleh berurusan dengan: skim tidak diberi kuasa; produk pelabur tidak dilesenkan; produk dalam senarai amaran.

#### Di bawah Prinsip 2 (¶4.3)

Tidak boleh:
- Menipu / aggresif dalam pengambilan Perunding.
- Bertindak bagi pelabur tanpa instrumen sah.
- **Menerima wang tunai / deposit tunai daripada pelabur.**
- **Amalan jualan agresif dan menyinggung perasaan.**
- Menerima cek dalam akaun selain Pengedar.
- Borang ditandatangan / cap ibu jari awal.
- Memberi mandat kepada bukan-orang berdaftar.
- **Memberi penyataan yang menimbulkan kekeliruan atau salah faham.**
- Diskaun tunai / rebat tunai.
- Churning (penebusan + pembelian berlebihan).
- **Menggunakan bahan iklan/promosi yang TIDAK MEMATUHI Garis Panduan Pengiklanan SC.**
- Penyataan negatif / tidak berasas tentang industri / FIMM / SC.
- **Menggalakkan pembiayaan pinjaman untuk melabur.**
- Mengguna logo FIMM tanpa kebenaran bertulis (kecuali untuk mendedahkan keahlian).

¶4.4 — Perunding **tidak** boleh:
- Menyamar Perunding lain.
- Menyerahkan jualan kepada Perunding lain.
- **Mengguna bahan iklan/promosi yang TIDAK DIBERIKAN ATAU DILULUSKAN OLEH Pengedarnya.**
- Mengguna kaedah penilaian selain yang diadopsi rasmi oleh Pengedar.
- Membuat keputusan pelaburan bagi pihak pelabur.
- Mengenakan yuran selain yang didedahkan dalam prospektus / PHS.

> **¶4.4(c) ialah peraturan pemasaran paling tajam:** setiap aset promosi yang dihasilkan oleh sistem agentik mesti melalui kelulusan Pengedar (Public Mutual) sebelum dianggap "bahan promosi diluluskan." Tanpa itu, Perunding melanggar Bab 4.

#### Di bawah Prinsip 3 (¶4.5)

Tiada switching demi komisen.

#### Di bawah Prinsip 4 (¶4.6)

Tidak boleh:
- **Memberi ramalan prestasi masa depan mana-mana Skim.**
- Membuat penyataan tentang yield/pulangan tanpa asas objektif & munasabah.
- **Menyembunyikan ciri yang berbeza apabila membandingkan Skim.**
- Memberi maklumat dalam bidang yang tidak kompeten.

> **¶4.6(a) adalah mutlak untuk ramalan dana tertentu.** Bersama §8.14 SC: ramalan kelas aset / pasaran umum boleh, ramalan dana tertentu tidak boleh.

#### Di bawah Prinsip 5 (¶4.7)

Tidak boleh mendedahkan / mengakses / menyebarkan maklumat pelabur tanpa kebenaran bertulis.

> **PDPA + Bab 4:** tangkapan skrin WhatsApp, akaun pelanggan, borang permohonan, portfolio — **tidak boleh dimuat naik secara awam**, walaupun dengan nama/nombor dikaburkan.

### Pendedahan profil yang diwajibkan

1. **Nombor pendaftaran FIMM** dipaparkan dalam semua saluran pemasaran (bio, penerangan saluran, profil setiap platform aktif).
2. **Tajuk "Public Mutual Unit Trust Consultant"** (dan "PRS Consultant" jika berkenaan).
3. **Tajuk dilarang (¶4.1(h)):** "Financial Planner", "Financial Advisor", "Investment Advisor", "Investment Consultant" — kecuali memegang CMSRL.
4. **Nama Pengedar (Public Mutual Berhad)** sepatutnya kelihatan dalam bio.
5. **Logo FIMM** hanya untuk mendedahkan keahlian/pendaftaran.

### Soalan terbuka

- **"Diberikan atau diluluskan" oleh Pengedar (¶4.4(c))** — bentuk kelulusan tidak ditetapkan. Andaian selamat: setiap aset kreatif yang dihasilkan agen memerlukan kelulusan; sistem mesti tambah medan "bukti kelulusan Pengedar" sebelum CreativeVariant boleh dianggap patuh.
- **Perkongsian semula kandungan korporat Public Mutual** — kemungkinan dibenarkan tanpa kelulusan berasingan (sudah "diberikan" oleh Pengedar) selagi tiada komentar tambahan yang mengubah makna.
