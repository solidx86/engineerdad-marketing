# PRS Default Option vs Self-Selected Funds

> Tax figures reflect publicly published LHDN/PPA schedules as of 2026-05-31; tax rules change annually — confirm the active year with LHDN and consult a tax adviser. Not personalised tax advice. The age-band default option categories (Growth/Moderate/Conservative) are drawn from publicly published SC/PPA guidelines; Public Mutual's specific fund mapping should be confirmed against the current PRS fund prospectus and PHS before advising.

## English

### The regulatory default option glide path

The SC's Guidelines on Private Retirement Schemes mandate a standardised "Default Option" — an automatic age-based allocation that applies to members who do not actively select their own fund. The glide path operates across three age bands:

| Age band | Default option category | Regulatory intent |
|---|---|---|
| Below 45 | Growth | Maximise long-run accumulation; higher equity exposure |
| 45–54 | Moderate | Begin de-risking; blended equity/income allocation |
| 55 and above | Conservative | Capital preservation orientation; lower equity weight |

At each age-band transition, a member who remains in the default option is automatically migrated to the appropriate category. Active self-selection overrides the default for that member's account.

### Four facts that matter for default vs self-select

- **The default option is a regulatory floor, not an optimised strategy.** It is designed to protect members who cannot or do not engage with fund selection. A parent with a 20-year horizon at age 35 who remains in the Growth default is not being harmed by the default — but they have also not assessed whether a specific Growth fund within the PRS universe better matches their return objective or cost structure.
- **"Growth", "Moderate", and "Conservative" are category labels, not individual fund names.** Within each category, PRS providers may offer multiple funds. Public Mutual's PRS range includes funds mapped to each of these categories (confirm the current fund lineup against the PRS prospectus); the default option selects the category, but the specific fund within each category is determined by the provider's own default designation.
- **A young parent who was enrolled in a legacy default at a period when the system had different category boundaries may be in an allocation that does not reflect their actual age band today.** Annual automatic migration only occurs if the system's records are current; member should verify their allocation category on the PPA member portal.
- **The Conservative category under-serves a 35-year-old whose account was incorrectly placed there.** The Conservative allocation is designed for near-retirees with a 0–5 year horizon. A 35-year-old forced into Conservative by an administrative error or mis-enrolment loses decades of growth potential. This is not a hypothetical — incorrect default enrolment has occurred when paper enrolment forms specified category incorrectly.
- **Self-selection allows the member to choose a higher-performing or lower-cost fund within the appropriate category.** The default option gives a "safe" allocation; it does not guarantee the best-performing fund in that category. An investor who reviews their PRS statement annually and actively matches their fund to their risk profile has the opportunity to improve outcomes within the same category.

### The Public Mutual PRS range

Public Mutual offers PRS funds mapped across Growth, Moderate, and Conservative categories. The specific funds, their investment mandates, and their applicable Fund Volatility Classes (FVCs) are published in the PRS prospectus and individual Product Highlights Sheets (PHS). Fund selection within a category should be verified against the current PHS — fund lineups and category assignments can change over time.

*Framing:* Most PRS members have never opened the PPA portal to check their allocation. For analytical parents, the framing is: the default option kept you safe from the worst outcome but did not do the work of selecting the best available fund for your horizon. That gap is the conversation.
