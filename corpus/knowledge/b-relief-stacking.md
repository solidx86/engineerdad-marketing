# PRS Tax Relief and Relief Stacking

> Tax figures reflect publicly published LHDN/PPA schedules as of 2026-05-31; tax rules change annually — confirm the active year with LHDN and consult a tax adviser. Not personalised tax advice. The RM3,000 PRS relief, the RM4,000 EPF+life shared cap, and the RM3,000 SSPN relief figures are drawn from the published LHDN relief schedule; the exact tax saved depends on the individual's marginal rate — confirm with a tax adviser before use in campaign copy.

## English

### What the LHDN relief schedule looks like for a saving parent

The Malaysian personal income tax return allows a set of named reliefs that reduce chargeable income before the marginal-rate table is applied. For a parent optimising retirement and education savings, the relevant clusters are:

| Relief line | Typical cap (RM) | Shared or standalone? |
|---|---|---|
| EPF contribution + life insurance premium | 7,000 combined (EPF up to 4,000; life up to 3,000) | **Shared** — both draw from one pool |
| PRS contribution | 3,000 | **Standalone** — its own separate line |
| SSPN net deposit (education savings) | 8,000 | Standalone |
| Education fees (own further study) | 7,000 (higher; some categories up to 17,000 — confirm LHDN) | Standalone |
| Medical / dental | 10,000 (combined with lifestyle; verify active year) | Varies by category |
| Lifestyle (books, internet, sports, devices) | 2,500 (additional categories vary) | Standalone |

*All caps and structures are drawn from publicly published LHDN schedules as of 2026-05-31; verify against the current Year of Assessment (YA) form before filing.*

### Four facts that matter for PRS stacking

- **PRS relief is a separate line item from the EPF+life shared pool.** A salaried employee who has already filled the RM4,000 EPF sub-cap still has a full RM3,000 PRS relief available. These are not competing for the same budget.
- **The EPF+life combined cap is RM7,000, with EPF sub-capped at RM4,000 and life at RM3,000 — but PRS sits entirely outside this.** Common misconception: investors assume PRS and EPF compete for the same RM4,000. They do not. The PRS line is governed by a separate provision.
- **PRS relief has a confirmed sunset at Year of Assessment 2030.** The relief was extended to YA 2030 under Budget 2025 (confirmed: ppa.my/prs-tax-relief/, fetched 2026-05-31). Consultants should verify whether any subsequent Budget extends or modifies this before presenting tax-saving projections to clients beyond YA 2030.
- **The marginal-rate dependency is the key variable.** A parent in the 13% bracket saves RM390 of tax on a full RM3,000 PRS contribution; at 24% the same RM3,000 saves RM720. The higher the chargeable income, the more valuable the relief — but only if chargeable income is high enough to use it.
- **SSPN (National Education Savings Scheme) stacks alongside PRS and EPF+life.** A parent contributing to all three — EPF (to the 4,000 sub-cap), PRS (RM3,000), and SSPN (up to RM8,000 net) — can claim reliefs across three separate lines simultaneously. The practical ceiling per parent is much higher than most people realise from a surface reading of the return form.

### The stacking optimisation logic

For a salaried parent already maxing EPF contributions through payroll deduction, the incremental relief action set is: (1) assess whether life insurance is filling the remaining RM3,000 in the EPF+life pool; (2) layer PRS contributions up to RM3,000 against its standalone line; (3) if education savings are in scope, add SSPN net deposits up to RM8,000. Each of these is independent — filling one does not reduce any other.

*Framing:* Most analytical parents have heard "PRS gives you a tax break" but few understand the structural reason: it is a standalone provision, not a sub-item of the EPF bucket. The relief-stacking map is a one-conversation piece that changes how they think about priority-ordering their annual contributions.
