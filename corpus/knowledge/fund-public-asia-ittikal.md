### Taxonomy notes

PAIF is the representative **regional equity** entry in the fund family. Key points for a consultant framing this fund:

- **Currency exposure:** as a predominantly foreign equity fund (94.5%), returns to a Malaysian investor are materially influenced by MYR versus KRW, TWD, JPY, and USD movements. In a year where Asian currencies weaken against MYR, the fund's MYR-denominated return will be lower than the local-currency return of the underlying portfolio. See `c-currency-exposure.md` for a full treatment.
- **Technology and semiconductor concentration:** the top-5 holdings (SK Hynix, Samsung, TSMC, Delta Electronics, HD Hyundai Electric) are heavily weighted to the global semiconductor supply chain. A sector-specific shock (e.g., US export controls, Taiwan Strait tension) could produce correlated drawdowns across these names simultaneously.
- **Shariah screening in a regional context:** the SC-approved Shariah screen applies across all geographies — holdings must comply regardless of their country of domicile. This does not guarantee a different geographic allocation from a conventional regional fund, but it may exclude certain financial or entertainment sector names that would otherwise appear in a conventional Asia ex-Japan portfolio.
- **Largest fund by AUM in the April 2026 snapshot** (RM 5.82B) — high AUM provides liquidity depth but also means the manager's execution footprint in some smaller Asian markets can be significant.
