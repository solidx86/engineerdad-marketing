# "Investing is for rich people, not someone like me"

> Describes the mechanics and trade-offs of small-balance investing; all RM figures are illustrative model output at a stated assumption, dated, and not a forecast or guaranteed outcome. Nothing here is personalised advice — read the relevant Master Prospectus and Product Highlights Sheet (PHS) before investing.

## English

### The myth

"Funds and investing — that's for people with money to spare, professionals in suits, the wealthy. Someone on my income isn't really the target. It's not built for people like me."

### The true part

There is a real grain of truth in the image. Plenty of products *are* gated — private banking, high-minimum mandates, advisers who only take six-figure portfolios. And historically, the standard entry point did ask for a meaningful lump sum. So the feeling that "this isn't built for me" comes from somewhere real: parts of the industry genuinely are built for the already-wealthy. The concession holds — the myth only errs in assuming the *entire* door is locked at that height.

### The variable it ignores

The ignored variable is the **actual minimum and the channel that sets it**. Through the online e-Series, the minimum initial and additional investment is **RM100** (corpus/knowledge/a-fee-schedule.md, e-Series Master Prospectus p. 60) — explicitly a tenth of the RM1,000 standard Public Series entry, which is what makes it accessible to first-time and lower-capital investors. The instrument is not reserved for the wealthy; the low-minimum channel exists precisely for everyone else.

And the people who most *need* it are not the rich — they are ordinary earners facing a retirement gap. The EPF shortfall cases show the cost of closing the same Adequate-tier gap is **small when started early and brutal when left late**: about RM408/month at age 25 versus more than an entire salary at age 50 (corpus/proof/epf-shortfall-cases.md). What modest, regular contributions build is the part the myth never sees: at an 8% illustrative return, RM100/month over 18 years projects to about RM48,000, RM200/month to about RM96,000, and RM300/month to about RM144,000 (corpus/data/charts/target-by-budget.yaml). Investing is the mechanism by which non-rich people close a gap — not a club for those who already have. Figures are illustrative; actual returns vary and past performance is not indicative of future results.

### Who it changes the answer for

For the genuinely cash-strapped household with no RM100 to redirect, the priority is cash flow and a buffer first — and that is honest, not a failing. For the far larger group who *do* have RM100 a month somewhere but have written themselves out because "investing is for the rich," the answer flips: the low-minimum channel was designed for exactly them, and the real cost is the years spent believing the door was closed.
