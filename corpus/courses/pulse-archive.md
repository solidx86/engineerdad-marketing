## English

### The three editorial buckets

**Market Directions** — read on US and global capital markets and what they mean for Malaysian families *today*. Not stock picks. Not predictions. Always tied back to the long-horizon parent investor: "what does this mean for an 18-year DCA plan?"

**Education Inflation** — the rising cost of degrees and how to outpace it before the child enrols at university. Anchors back to the canonical numbers (RM40k–60k → RM100k+ over 18 years).

**Wealth Concepts** — macro wealth-building ideas explained simply for parents wanting to build generational financial literacy. The most "engineering manual" of the three buckets — high explainer density.

### Format conventions

- **Length** — short. Pulse is the brand's *fast* channel; long-form lives in articles/.
- **Voice** — first-person, conversational expert. "I've been watching X this week — here's what it means."
- **Tone** — calm, anti-hype. The Pulse never says "buy this" or "panic". It diagnoses.
- **Numbers** — every Pulse entry has at least one specific number (a price level, a yield, a year-on-year change, a survey figure).
- **Closing line** — bring it back to the long horizon: "for an 18-year DCA plan, this is noise" / "this is signal" / "this is a setup worth annual review."
- **Disclaimer** — every entry closes with the educational-purposes line.

### Seeded entry — *How to Start a Children's Education Fund: A 5-Step Guide*

Published 6 May 2026 · 7-minute read · engineerdad.my/articles/start-childrens-education-fund-malaysia.html

> *"Most Malaysian parents I speak with already know they should be investing for their child's education. The block isn't motivation — it's the gap between intention and the actual mechanics. This guide closes that gap. Five steps, in the order I walk every new client through."*

The five steps:
1. Define the goal in numbers.
2. Pick the right fund category.
3. Open the account in your child's name.
4. Set up monthly auto-debit (DCA).
5. Review annually, not monthly.

Plus four canonical "starter mistakes":
- Waiting for the "right time."
- Starting too aggressively.
- Watching daily fund prices.
- Mixing the fund with other goals.
