# Securities Commission Malaysia — Marketing & Advertising Compliance

> Compliance grounding for AI marketing agents producing content on behalf of a licensed Public Mutual Unit Trust Consultant + PRS Consultant. Authority: Securities Commission Malaysia (SC) under section 377 of the Capital Markets and Services Act 2007 (CMSA). Primary instrument: **Guidelines on Advertising for Capital Market Products and Related Services SC-GL/ADV-2020 (R1-2025)**, issued 4 May 2020, revised 27 March 2025, **effective 1 November 2025** (the "SC Advertising Guidelines"). Supplementary: SC FAQs (Revised 27 March 2025) and FIMM's Guidance Notes on the SC Advertising Guidelines (Revised 26 September 2025).

## English

### Governing references

- **Capital Markets and Services Act 2007 (CMSA)** — section 377 empowers the SC to issue marketing/advertising guidelines binding on capital market intermediaries.
- **SC Advertising Guidelines SC-GL/ADV-2020 (R1-2025)** — issued 4 May 2020, revised 27 March 2025, effective 1 November 2025.
- **SC FAQs (Revised 27 March 2025)** — operational interpretations and worked examples (27 questions).
- **FIMM Guidance Notes (Revised 26 September 2025)** — UTS/PRS-specific interpretations, with Tables A–C of compliant vs non-compliant statements.

These layered rules are the SC baseline. FIMM (`fimm.md`) adds Code-of-Ethics duties for Registered Persons; Public Mutual (`public-mutual.md`) adds principal-specific rules.

### Who is bound (SC §2.01, §4.01)

The Guidelines apply to every "advertiser" — any of:

1. An **issuer** of a capital market product (e.g., Public Mutual as the management company).
2. A **capital market intermediary** — a CMSL holder, a CMSRL holder, a registered person under section 76 CMSA, or a person registered under section 76A.
3. A person operating or maintaining a recognised market.
4. A **voluntary advertiser** — any person not engaged by (1)–(3) who on their own accord issues or authorises an advertisement, **including finfluencers**.

A licensed Public Mutual UTC + PRS Consultant is a **registered person under section 76 CMSA / capital market intermediary** for SC purposes. Every public-channel post, video, article, livestream, or DM that promotes a Public Mutual fund or PRS scheme — directly or by inducement — is an advertisement.

> **Voluntary-advertiser carveout (SC §2.01 Guidance, FAQ Q6):** content that is "solely educational" and does **not** induce a person to take any action or position (buy / sell / hold) on a specific product is not caught. Naming a specific fund, recommending action, or implying it tilts the post into "advertisement" status. Generic literacy content (compounding, inflation, asset allocation) is generally outside scope **provided** there is no buy-now / pitch element.

### Channels in scope (SC §2.03)

> Magazines and newspapers; broadcasts (radio, television, cinema); display-only materials (billboards, signs, posters, exhibition panels, transit ads); the internet, including webpages, banner ads, video streaming (YouTube), social networking platforms (Facebook, LinkedIn), microblogging (X, Threads); social media and internet discussion sites; mobile messages and messaging apps (SMS, MMS, WhatsApp); product/service brochures and promotional fact sheets (e.g., fund fact sheets); promotional activities; direct mail (post, fax, email); telemarketing and on-hold audio; presentations, seminars, advertorials.

> **FAQ Q24:** "re-posting or sharing content that seeks to promote a capital market product or capital market-related service constitutes an advertisement and such advertisement must comply with the Guidelines." Reshares of a Public Mutual post by a UTC are advertisements.

### Five core principles for every advertisement (SC §5.01–§5.06)

1. **Clear, relevant and balanced** information about the product or service (§5.01a).
2. **Current and accurate** information so far as reasonably practicable (§5.01b). Stale information must be corrected, withdrawn, or replaced; QR codes pointing to outdated material must be deactivated (App.1 B1).
3. **No misleading or deceptive messaging.** "An advertiser must ensure that the advertisement must not contain any messaging that is likely to mislead or deceive an investor, such as an exaggerated, flamboyant, overstated, or over-zealous messaging" (§5.02 — verbatim).
4. **Fair treatment; no exploitation of investor vulnerability.** §5.03 prohibits exploiting "behavioural traits, financial status, lack of expertise, experience or knowledge." §5.04 requires due regard for vulnerable segments (elderly, persons with disability) and accessible formats.
5. **Identifiability.** §5.05 requires "the intended target audience to immediately identify [the content] as an advertisement." §5.06 prohibits advertising services of any person not licensed/registered/authorised by the SC.

### Prohibited content — Red Flag Phrasing

The SC bans specific phrasing types under §5.02 (no exaggerated/flamboyant/overstated messaging) and Appendix 1 §K (no terms or phrases that "create expectations that cannot be met", "imply features which do not exist", or "make assertions that are not verified"). FIMM's GN Table A lists the canonical UTS/PRS examples — DO NOT USE any of these or close variants:

**A. Exaggerated / unverified superlatives (FIMM GN Table A1):**
- "Our Fund is the best performing fund in Malaysia."
- "Invest with us, the best performing fund manager in Asia."
- "the best", "most popular", "#1 fund", "top-rated" — unless backed by an independent third-party ranking within a reasonable validity period (e.g., one year for an annual award).

**B. Statements that downplay risk (FIMM GN Table A2):**
- "Your returns are guaranteed."
- "Invest with zero risk!"
- Words: **"secure", "guaranteed", "safe", "risk-free", "warranty", "promise", "zero risk", "no risk"** — banned unless inclusion has a reasonable basis (FIMM GN ¶2.5 B2).
- **"save" / "savings"** — avoided when describing investments (FIMM GN ¶2.5 B2 final bullet) because UTS/PRS are not savings products.

**C. Get-rich-quick / overzealous returns (FIMM GN Table A3):**
- "Your investment will grow from strength to strength."
- "Greater wealth awaits you."
- "You too can be rich with one simple step."
- "High-flying returns awaits you."
- "Get rich quick" framings are banned outright.

**D. Urgency / FOMO (FIMM GN Table A4):**
- "Invest now before it is too late."
- "Buy now before it's too late."
- "Hurry, offer period ends on…"
- "This is the best time to invest."
- "This chance will not happen that often."
- "This golden opportunity to invest now only happen once in your lifetime."
- "Those who had invested in the past crisis have made good returns and you should invest now."

> **Why these are banned (FIMM GN ¶2.5 D2):** "Investors should not be pressured into investing within a certain time frame or by a certain time, as this may impede their ability to conduct their own research and due diligence into the investment product."

**E. Capital / yield guarantees (SC §8.08, verbatim):**
> Where the principal sum or rate of returns is not guaranteed, an advertisement must NOT state that the risk of losing principal is "low or nil" or that the risk of not achieving the stated/target/expected returns is "low or nil."

**F. Misleading thematic labels (SC FAQ Example 2):** Labelling a fund "green" / "Shariah" / "ESG" / "income" when the underlying portfolio does not actually meet that mandate.

**G. Unauthorised / out-of-scope products (SC §5.06, FAQ Example 9):** Advertising forex platforms, crypto exchanges, individual stock-picking services, day-trading signals, structured products of unauthorised foreign brokers — even by reshare — is a breach. The consultant's licence covers Public Mutual unit trusts and PRS schemes; promoting anything else under that licensed identity is an SC violation.

**H. SC logo / endorsement implication (SC §8.16–§8.18, FAQ Example 6):**
- An advertisement must clearly state it has not been reviewed by the SC.
- Must not use the SC logo or imply SC endorsement.
- If the disclosure document was registered/lodged with the SC, the advertisement must include the boilerplate that registration "does not amount to nor indicate that the SC has recommended or endorsed" the product.

### Performance-data rules (SC §8.09, App.1 §H)

- **Returns must be net of fees, charges, costs** (§8.09a). Effect of fees on returns must be explained, not hidden (§8.09b).
- **Foreign-currency returns must be flagged as such** (§8.10).
- **Past performance must be sourced from official documents** — Master Prospectus, Product Highlights Sheet (PHS), official Fund Fact Sheet. Custom screeners (e.g., the Alpha-filtered Monthly Fund Review on engineerdad.my) are derivative analyses and must be framed as **educational market analysis**, not a buy-list (FIMM CoE ¶4.6b — no statement on yields or returns "without any objective and reasonable basis").
- **No cherry-picking timeframes** (App.1 §H2): "any historical period used for comparison or relating to past performance should not be chosen solely to present a favourable impression on the advertised product or service. For example, the historical period used should not be an odd period (e.g. 5 years and 2 months) for the sole purpose of capturing a positive period."
- **Mandatory caveat on every past-performance reference** (App.1 §H1, FAQ Q18): the advertisement must "draw attention to the fact that the past performance is not indicative of future performance."
- **Benchmark comparisons** (App.1 §H3): the comparison must be relevant and the basis for the comparison must be disclosed.

### Forward-looking / forecast statements (SC §8.14, App.1 §H4, FIMM GN ¶6.1)

Forecasts are permitted only if all five conditions hold:

1. There is a **reasonable basis** for the forecast (§8.14, FIMM GN ¶6.1a).
2. The advertisement clearly states the forecast **may not occur** (FIMM GN ¶6.1b).
3. The forecast is **not stated in certain terms** — uncertainty must be acknowledged (FIMM GN ¶6.1c).
4. The **basis and assumptions** behind the forecast are stated (FIMM GN ¶6.1d, App.1 §H4).
5. Assumptions are made readily available to investors and grounded in credible market research (FIMM GN ¶6.1e).

> **FIMM CoE ¶4.6a (verbatim):** "A Distributor and Consultant shall not… provide forecast of future performance of any Scheme."
>
> Read FIMM CoE ¶4.6a together with SC §8.14: a UTC may not forecast a *specific Scheme's* future performance. General market or asset-class forecasts (with a reasonable basis and the five conditions above) are permitted. Specific fund forecasts are not.

### Comparisons (SC §8.15, App.1 §I)

- Comparisons of products, benefits, or returns must be **accurate, balanced, and have a reasonable basis** (§8.15).
- Compared products must have **sufficiently similar features and context** (App.1 §I1a). Differences must be carefully assessed and clearly disclosed.
- **No undue prominence** to features that downplay other relevant features of the comparator (App.1 §I1b).
- **Limitations and qualifications** of the comparison must be clearly disclosed — including a short comparison window if used (App.1 §I1c).
- **Verified data + assumptions disclosed** (App.1 §I2).

**Ratings, rankings, awards (App.1 §I3–§I5):**
- Only credible sources (credit ratings, established research houses).
- Explain meaning, scale, source, and currentness.
- State that ratings/rankings are not a sole basis for an investment decision.
- For awards: identify the grantor, explain the award, disclose if the grantor is related to the advertiser.

### EPF / KWSP comparison rule (consultant-internal layered rule)

SC §8.15 governs all comparisons. Layered on top by Public Mutual's internal rulebook: any EPF-vs-unit-trust framing must be balanced. Never claim or imply that unit trusts are "safer" or "guaranteed to beat" EPF dividends. Acknowledge that **EPF provides a guaranteed minimum dividend** (Employees Provident Fund Act 1991 §27). Failing to acknowledge that minimum is a §8.15 balance breach.

### Testimonials & endorsements (SC §8.05, App.1 — Conflict of Interest, FIMM GN ¶3.1–§3.5)

- Testimonials/endorsements must be from someone who **actually invested in the advertised product or used the advertised service** (§8.05).
- Each testimonial must be accompanied by a clear statement disclosing **whether the person is being compensated** by the advertiser (§8.05).
- **Voluntary advertisers and third-party providers** must disclose any direct or indirect benefits (§8.06).
- **Public-figure / celebrity / influencer endorsements** require additional controls (FIMM GN ¶3.2): higher-ranked approval, signed declaration of compliance, optional minimum exclusivity period.
- Testimonials must be **factual, truthful, reasonably held informed opinions** (FIMM GN ¶3.4).
- A testimonial referencing a fund's performance must include the **source and date** (FIMM GN Table A1, last bullet).

> **PDPA + FIMM CoE overlay:** the consultant must not post WhatsApp screenshots, customer-account screenshots, application forms, or any other private client material — even with names blurred — under FIMM CoE ¶3.31, FIMM CoE ¶4.7, and PDPA 2010. Hypothetical case studies clearly marked as hypothetical are permitted.

### Suitability statements & target audience (SC §8.01, App.1 §A, FIMM GN ¶4.1–§4.7, FAQ Q25–Q26)

- An advertisement must not state or imply that a product is suitable for a particular group **unless** the advertiser has actually assessed suitability for that group (App.1 §A1).
- For products restricted to **sophisticated investors**, the ad must say so AND the advertiser must take active steps (e.g., geo-blocking, gated platforms) to keep non-sophisticated investors from accessing it. **A disclaimer alone is not sufficient** (FAQ Q25).
- Public Mutual funds restricted to sophisticated investors (e.g., funds offered under the Lodge-and-Launch framework) must **never** be promoted on open social media where audience can't be verified.
- The consultant's licensed identity must perform a documented **Suitability Assessment Form (SAF)** before recommending a specific scheme. Public-channel posts must not function as a sales-pipeline shortcut around the SAF process. Specific fund recommendations are reserved for **private one-on-one consultations**.

### Mandatory disclaimers — canonical forms

The SC Guidelines do **not prescribe** disclaimer wording but require advertisers to inform investors that the ad / product is subject to relevant warnings, disclaimers, qualifications, terms (§8.13), and that investors should:

> (i) read and understand the disclosure document; (ii) understand the risks; (iii) compare and consider fees, charges, costs; (iv) make their own risk assessment and seek professional advice (App.1 §G1d).

Public Mutual's canonical disclaimer text (sourced from publicmutual.com.my/pmb/advertising-disclaimer and /investment-disclaimer; see `public-mutual.md` for verbatim) bundles these and is the safe default. Two short-form variants for our content:

**Short-form / general posts (text caption):**
> Disclaimer: This content is for educational purposes only and does not constitute personalised financial advice. Investors are advised to read and understand the contents of the relevant Master Prospectus and Product Highlights Sheet (PHS) before investing. Past performance of a fund is not a reliable indicator of its future performance.

**Long-form / deep-dive analytical content (YouTube description + on-screen):**
> Regulatory & Risk Disclaimer: The information and performance metrics discussed in this video are for educational purposes only and should not be construed as a recommendation to buy or sell any specific unit trust fund or private retirement scheme. Investors are advised to read and understand the contents of the official Master Prospectus, Supplemental Prospectus (if any), Information Memorandum (if any), and Product Highlights Sheet (PHS) before making any investment decision. Unit trust and PRS investments are subject to market risks; the value of units and any distributions payable may go down as well as up. Past performance, including any historical returns or alpha generation referenced, is not a reliable indicator of future performance. The contents of this video have not been reviewed by the Securities Commission Malaysia.

**Disclaimer presentation rules (App.1 §G1, FIMM GN ¶5.4):**
- Presented "as closely as possible to the main message of the advertisement."
- Sufficient prominence — font size ≥ 10-point Times New Roman or visually equivalent (FIMM GN ¶7.7a).
- Easily understood by investors — simple language (FIMM GN ¶5.4a).
- Sufficient colour contrast for legibility (FIMM GN ¶7.7b).
- For audio/video: read at a pace an average listener can understand (App.2 §A1, §B2; FIMM GN ¶5.4c).
- For Instagram-style truncation: key risks and Ts&Cs must NOT be hidden behind an "…more" ellipsis (FAQ Example 8).

### Channel-specific rules (SC §8.19–§8.20, App.2, FIMM GN ¶7.1–§7.7)

- **Choose channel appropriately.** Complex products on character-limited media (X, TikTok captions) may be inappropriate (App.1 §D1–D2, App.2 §C3).
- **Social media** is permitted (§8.20) provided the advertiser has assessed appropriateness, specificities, limitations, and risks. FIMM GN explicitly limits "Social Media (Corporate Accounts Only)" to "Avoid direct product recommendations in public posts; use for educational or promotional content with disclaimers" (FIMM GN ¶7.3 E).
- **Hyperlinks/QR codes** can supplement but cannot fix a misleading overall impression (App.2 §C2). Outdated QR codes must be deactivated (App.1 §B1).
- **Outdoor advertising** is suited to brand recognition only, not complex product information (App.2 §D2).
- **Audio/video and on-hold messages** are subject to the same rules; warnings must be read at a comprehensible pace (App.2 §A1–B2).

### Finfluencer rules (R1-2025 — newly added; FAQ Q4 Example, FIMM GN preamble)

> "X, who is a famous YouTuber and TikTok influencer, voluntarily advertises the investment products of Company B on his social media account without being engaged by Company B. The revised Guidelines will apply to X as he will qualify as an advertiser under paragraph (e)…" (FAQ Q4 illustration).

- Voluntary advertisers (including finfluencers) must comply with **all** principles and content rules (§5–§8).
- They must **disclose direct or indirect benefits** received (§8.06).
- Hashtags such as `#advertisement`, `#sponsored`, `#paidpartnership` are permissible to identify content as an ad provided the disclosure is clear and easily understood (FAQ Q21–Q22).
- An influencer who promotes a non-SC-licensed platform commits a §5.06 breach **and** may be prosecuted for abetting an unauthorised activity (FAQ Example 9).

### Third-party providers & supervisor accountability (SC §6.01–§6.02, §7.01–§7.02, FIMM GN ¶3.5)

- Advertiser's **board** must ensure effective control, policies, procedures (CPP) for compliance (§6.01) and review them regularly (§6.02).
- An advertiser using a third-party provider remains **responsible for the third party's conduct** (§7.01).
- Effective oversight (App.1 §C1–C2): records of third-party identity, social-media accounts used, content review before publication, ongoing monitoring.
- For UTC organisations (Distributor + Consultant): the **Distributor** is the supervisor on the hook for the Consultant's marketing under §7.02 + FIMM CoE ¶3.19. Pre-publication sign-off should be in place where the Consultant publishes promotional material on behalf of, or referencing, the Distributor.

### Consistency with disclosure document (SC §8.02–§8.04)

- Every ad must be consistent with the Master Prospectus / Product Highlights Sheet / relevant agreements.
- Where relevant, the ad must direct investors to the disclosure document, state where it can be found, and the issuance date.
- The disclosure document must be readily available and easily accessible. As a voluntary advertiser, the consultant must inform the audience where to find the documents (FAQ Q27).

### Promotional terms (SC §8.13, App.1 §M)

- If a campaign includes a limited-period promotion (discounts, gifts), all warnings, disclaimers, qualifications, and material Ts&Cs must be clearly stated.
- Indicate the promotional period's duration.
- Prizes / gifts must not obscure investment risk (FAQ Example 3 — luxury car prize without prominent T&Cs is non-compliant).

### Title restrictions

A licensed UTC + PRS Consultant is **not** authorised to use:
- "Financial Planner" — requires SC-issued Capital Markets Services Representative's Licence (CMSRL) for financial planning.
- "Financial Advisor" — same.
- "Investment Advisor" / "Investment Consultant" — restricted by FIMM CoE ¶4.1h unless authorised by the relevant regulatory authorities.

Permissible titles: "Public Mutual Unit Trust Consultant", "PRS Consultant", "UTC", "Registered Person" (FIMM-registered).

> **FIMM CoE ¶4.1h (verbatim):** "A Distributor and Consultant shall not… use, or by inference adopt or display, the designation, title or qualification of 'financial planner', 'financial advisor', 'investment advisor' or 'investment consultant' unless authorised by the relevant regulatory authorities or professional bodies."

### Record-keeping & internal policies (SC §6.01, FAQ Q14)

Internal written policies must include:
1. Internal approval and reporting processes.
2. Assessment criteria to ensure compliance with the Guidelines.
3. Record keeping (advertisements, suitability assessments, sign-off trails).

### Enforcement & cautionary precedent

The Guidelines are issued under section 377 CMSA and binding on advertisers. Non-compliance can lead to SC-level enforcement (administrative actions under section 354 CMSA, public reprimand) and CMSA-level prosecution (e.g., §369 — false or misleading statements; abetting under §370 — see FAQ Example 9). FIMM separately disciplines Registered Persons under FIMM Consolidated Rules Chapter 6 — see `fimm.md`.

The 2025 revision was driven by the rising prominence of social media and finfluencers (SC media release, 27 Mar 2025; SC FAQ Q2). FAQ Examples 7–9 are cautionary illustrations targeted at influencer activity:
- **Example 7:** Risks disclosed in the caption but not the video body — non-compliant; risks must be in the main message.
- **Example 8:** Risks hidden behind "…more" ellipsis on Instagram — non-compliant; trader must consider whether the platform is suitable.
- **Example 9:** Influencer promoting an SG-licensed broker not authorised by SC Malaysia — both an SC §5.06 breach and CMSA abetting offence.

### Open questions / ambiguities

- **Reposting nuance** (FAQ Q24): a consultant resharing a Public Mutual corporate post is an advertisement and inherits responsibility — but the corporate poster has already complied. In practice, the safer path is to add the consultant's own disclaimer/credential bio to the share so the SC's three pillars (clear/balanced/risk-warning) remain visible to the consultant's specific audience.
- **Educational vs promotional line** (FAQ Q6 Examples): generic explainers about compounding, asset allocation, EPF, PRS structure are out of scope. Naming a specific fund, ranking funds with a "buy" implication, or quoting a specific fund's performance with a "you should invest" tilt brings the post into scope. Borderline content should be reviewed under §5.01 ("clear, relevant, balanced") before publishing.
- **Hashtag adequacy** (FAQ Q21): `#advertisement` alone may be insufficient if the rest of the post still implies factual/educational content. The disclosure should be unambiguous to the **target audience**, not just present.

### Skop

Garis panduan ini terpakai kepada setiap "pengiklan", termasuk:
1. Penerbit produk pasaran modal (mis. Public Mutual).
2. Pengantara pasaran modal (pemegang CMSL/CMSRL, orang berdaftar di bawah seksyen 76 atau 76A CMSA).
3. Operator pasaran yang diiktiraf.
4. **Pengiklan secara sukarela** — termasuk pempengaruh kewangan (finfluencer) yang mengiklan secara bebas.

Perunding Public Mutual UTC + PRS yang berlesen ialah **orang berdaftar di bawah seksyen 76 CMSA**. Setiap siaran awam, video, artikel, atau siaran langsung yang mempromosikan dana Public Mutual atau skim PRS — secara langsung atau secara siratan — adalah iklan.

> **Pengecualian pengiklan sukarela (FAQ Q6):** kandungan yang **semata-mata pendidikan** dan **tidak** mendorong sesiapa membeli/menjual/memegang produk tertentu adalah di luar skop. Kandungan literasi am (faedah berganda, inflasi, peruntukan aset) selamat selagi tiada elemen jualan.

### Saluran yang tertakluk (SC §2.03)

Akhbar dan majalah; siaran (radio, TV, pawagam); media papan tanda (papan iklan, pamflet, eksibisi, transit); internet (laman web, sepanduk, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, X, Threads); media sosial dan tapak perbincangan; mesej telefon mudah alih (SMS, MMS, WhatsApp); risalah produk dan helaian fakta promosi; aktiviti promosi; mel terus (pos, faks, e-mel); telepemasaran dan mesej audio panggilan tertahan; pembentangan, seminar, advertorial.

> **FAQ Q24:** "perkongsian semula kandungan yang bertujuan mempromosikan produk atau perkhidmatan pasaran modal" juga dikira sebagai iklan.

### Lima prinsip teras (SC §5.01–§5.06)

1. **Maklumat jelas, relevan, dan seimbang** tentang produk atau perkhidmatan.
2. **Maklumat semasa dan tepat** sejauh praktikal — maklumat lapuk mesti dibetulkan, ditarik balik, atau diganti.
3. **Tiada mesej mengelirukan atau menipu** — termasuk mesej yang berlebih-lebihan, flamboyant, atau "over-zealous".
4. **Layanan adil; tiada eksploitasi kelemahan pelabur** — emosi, status kewangan, kekurangan pengalaman/pengetahuan.
5. **Boleh dikenal pasti sebagai iklan** dengan serta-merta. Tiada iklan untuk perkhidmatan pihak yang tidak dilesenkan/didaftarkan oleh SC.

### Senarai Perkataan Bendera Merah — JANGAN GUNA

Berdasarkan Jadual A Nota Panduan FIMM:

**A. Penyataan keterlaluan / superlatif tidak terbukti:**
- "Dana kami adalah dana berprestasi terbaik di Malaysia."
- "Pengurus dana terbaik di Asia."
- "terbaik", "paling popular", "#1", "berkadar tertinggi" — kecuali disokong oleh ranking pihak ketiga bebas dalam tempoh sah munasabah (mis. satu tahun untuk anugerah tahunan).

**B. Penyataan yang mengecilkan risiko:**
- "Pulangan anda dijamin."
- "Labur dengan risiko sifar!"
- Perkataan: **"selamat", "dijamin / terjamin", "risiko-sifar", "tiada risiko", "jaminan", "janji"** — dilarang melainkan ada justifikasi munasabah.
- Perkataan **"simpanan / menyimpan"** — elakkan, kerana pelaburan amanah saham bukan produk simpanan.

**C. Penyataan cepat-kaya / pulangan berlebih-lebihan:**
- "Pelaburan anda akan terus berkembang dengan kuat."
- "Kekayaan lebih besar menanti anda."
- "Anda juga boleh kaya dengan satu langkah mudah."
- "Pulangan tinggi terbang menanti anda."
- "Cepat kaya" — dilarang sepenuhnya.

**D. Tekanan masa / FOMO:**
- "Labur sekarang sebelum terlambat."
- "Beli sekarang sebelum lambat."
- "Cepat, tempoh tawaran berakhir pada…"
- "Ini masa terbaik untuk melabur."
- "Peluang ini tidak akan berulang."
- "Peluang emas yang hanya muncul sekali seumur hidup."

> **Mengapa dilarang (Nota Panduan FIMM ¶2.5 D2):** "Pelabur tidak sepatutnya ditekan untuk melabur dalam jangka masa tertentu kerana ini menghalang mereka melakukan penyiasatan wajar terhadap produk pelaburan."

**E. Jaminan modal / pulangan (SC §8.08):** Apabila modal atau kadar pulangan tidak dijamin, iklan TIDAK BOLEH menyatakan bahawa risiko kerugian modal "rendah atau sifar" atau bahawa risiko tidak mencapai pulangan sasaran "rendah atau sifar".

**F. Label tematik mengelirukan (SC FAQ Contoh 2):** Melabel dana sebagai "hijau" / "Shariah" / "ESG" / "pendapatan" sedangkan portfolio asas tidak memenuhi mandat itu.

**G. Produk di luar skop / tidak diberi kuasa (SC §5.06):** Mengiklan platform forex, kripto, pemilihan saham individu, perdagangan harian, atau produk berstruktur broker asing tidak berlesen — termasuk melalui perkongsian semula — adalah pelanggaran. Lesen perunding meliputi dana amanah saham Public Mutual dan skim PRS sahaja.

**H. Logo SC / siratan sokongan (SC §8.16–§8.18):**
- Iklan mesti menyatakan dengan jelas bahawa ia tidak disemak oleh SC.
- Tidak boleh menggunakan logo SC atau menyiratkan SC menyokong/mengiktiraf.
- Penyataan baku: "Pendaftaran/pemfailan dokumen pendedahan dengan SC tidak bermakna SC mengesyorkan atau menyokong dana tersebut."

### Peraturan data prestasi (SC §8.09, App.1 §H)

- **Pulangan mesti bersih daripada yuran/caj/kos.**
- **Pulangan mata wang asing mesti dinyatakan sedemikian.**
- **Sumber rasmi sahaja** — Prospektus Induk, Helaian Sorotan Produk (PHS), Helaian Fakta Dana rasmi. Penapis tersuai ialah analisis pendidikan, bukan senarai beli.
- **Larangan memilih jangka masa** (App.1 §H2): tempoh sejarah tidak boleh dipilih semata-mata untuk memberi kesan baik (mis. tempoh 5 tahun 2 bulan yang ganjil).
- **Penafian wajib pada setiap rujukan prestasi:** "Prestasi lalu sesebuah dana bukan petunjuk yang boleh dipercayai bagi prestasi masa depan."
- **Perbandingan tanda aras** (App.1 §H3): mesti relevan dan asas perbandingan didedahkan.

### Penyataan ramalan / unjuran (SC §8.14, FIMM Nota Panduan ¶6.1)

Lima syarat:
1. Asas munasabah.
2. Iklan menyatakan ramalan **mungkin tidak berlaku**.
3. Tidak dinyatakan dalam terma muktamad.
4. Asas dan andaian dinyatakan.
5. Andaian tersedia kepada pelabur dan berasaskan kajian pasaran kredibel.

> **FIMM CoE ¶4.6a:** "Pengedar dan Perunding tidak boleh… memberi ramalan prestasi masa depan bagi mana-mana Skim." Membaca §8.14 SC bersama ¶4.6a FIMM: ramalan **dana tertentu** dilarang. Ramalan kelas aset / pasaran umum dengan asas munasabah dibenarkan.

### Perbandingan (SC §8.15, App.1 §I)

- Mesti tepat, seimbang, dan ada asas munasabah.
- Produk yang dibandingkan mesti mempunyai ciri yang cukup serupa.
- Tiada penonjolan tidak adil terhadap ciri yang mengabaikan ciri lain.
- Had / kelayakan perbandingan didedahkan dengan jelas.

**Penilaian, ranking, anugerah:** Hanya sumber kredibel; jelaskan makna, skala, sumber, ketersediaan terkini; nyatakan ranking bukan asas tunggal keputusan pelaburan.

### Peraturan perbandingan KWSP

§8.15 mengawal semua perbandingan. Lapisan dalaman Public Mutual menambah: perbandingan KWSP-lawan-amanah-saham mesti seimbang. Jangan dakwa atau sirat amanah saham "lebih selamat" atau "dijamin mengatasi" dividen KWSP. Akui bahawa **KWSP memberikan dividen minimum yang dijamin** (Akta KWSP 1991 §27). Tidak mengakui dividen minimum tersebut adalah pelanggaran prinsip seimbang §8.15.

### Testimoni & sokongan (SC §8.05–§8.06, FIMM Nota Panduan ¶3.1–§3.5)

- Testimoni hanya daripada pelabur sebenar / pengguna perkhidmatan sebenar.
- Mesti disertai pernyataan jelas sama ada penyumbang testimoni dibayar / mendapat manfaat.
- Pengiklan secara sukarela dan pihak ketiga mesti mendedahkan manfaat langsung atau tidak langsung.
- Endorsemen tokoh / selebriti / pempengaruh memerlukan kawalan tambahan: kelulusan peringkat lebih tinggi, akuan bertulis, kemungkinan tempoh eksklusif minimum.

> **PDPA + FIMM CoE:** Tidak boleh memuat naik tangkapan skrin WhatsApp, akaun pelanggan, borang permohonan, atau bahan klien peribadi — walaupun nama dikaburkan. Kes hipotetikal yang dilabel jelas dibenarkan.

### Khalayak sasaran & kesesuaian (SC §8.01, App.1 §A, FIMM Nota Panduan ¶4.1–§4.7)

- Tidak boleh menyatakan / siratkan produk sesuai untuk kumpulan tertentu melainkan kesesuaian benar-benar dinilai.
- Untuk produk pelabur canggih: iklan mesti menyatakan demikian DAN pengiklan mengambil langkah aktif (geo-blok, platform berdaftar) untuk mengelak akses pelabur bukan-canggih.
- Dana Public Mutual yang dikhususkan untuk pelabur canggih **tidak boleh** dipromosikan di media sosial terbuka.
- Identiti perunding berlesen mesti melaksanakan **Borang Penilaian Kesesuaian (SAF)** sebelum mengesyorkan skim tertentu. Saluran awam tidak boleh menjadi jalan pintas SAF; cadangan dana tertentu hanya untuk **perundingan persendirian satu-dengan-satu**.

### Penafian wajib — bentuk baku

**Pos pendek / am (kapsyen teks):**
> Penafian: Kandungan ini adalah untuk tujuan pendidikan sahaja dan bukan nasihat kewangan peribadi. Pelabur dinasihatkan membaca dan memahami kandungan Prospektus Induk dan Helaian Sorotan Produk (PHS) yang berkaitan sebelum membuat pelaburan. Prestasi lalu sesebuah dana bukan petunjuk yang boleh dipercayai bagi prestasi masa depan.

**Pos panjang / analisis mendalam (penerangan YouTube + di-skrin):**
> Penafian Kawal Selia & Risiko: Maklumat dan metrik prestasi dalam video ini adalah untuk tujuan pendidikan sahaja dan tidak boleh ditafsirkan sebagai cadangan untuk membeli atau menjual mana-mana dana amanah saham atau skim persaraan persendirian tertentu. Pelabur dinasihatkan membaca dan memahami kandungan Prospektus Induk rasmi, Prospektus Tambahan (jika ada), Memorandum Maklumat (jika ada), dan Helaian Sorotan Produk (PHS) sebelum membuat sebarang keputusan pelaburan. Pelaburan amanah saham dan PRS tertakluk kepada risiko pasaran; nilai unit dan agihan yang dibayar boleh menurun dan juga meningkat. Prestasi lalu, termasuk pulangan sejarah atau penjanaan alpha yang dirujuk, bukan petunjuk yang boleh dipercayai bagi prestasi masa depan. Kandungan video ini tidak disemak oleh Suruhanjaya Sekuriti Malaysia.

**Peraturan persembahan penafian:**
- Diletakkan sedekat mungkin dengan mesej utama.
- Saiz fon ≥ 10-mata Times New Roman atau setara.
- Bahasa mudah; kontras warna mencukupi.
- Untuk audio/video: dibaca pada kelajuan yang difahami pendengar purata.
- Untuk Instagram-truncation: risiko utama tidak boleh disembunyikan di sebalik "…lebih lagi" (FAQ Contoh 8).

### Peraturan saluran (SC §8.19–§8.20, App.2)

- Pilih saluran yang sesuai. Produk kompleks pada media beratasan aksara (X, kapsyen TikTok) mungkin tidak sesuai.
- Media sosial dibenarkan dengan penilaian kesesuaian, kekhususan, had, dan risiko.
- Hyperlink/QR boleh tambah tetapi tidak membaiki tanggapan keseluruhan yang mengelirukan.
- Pengiklanan luar (papan iklan): pengiktirafan jenama sahaja, bukan maklumat produk kompleks.
- Audio/video: amaran dibaca pada kelajuan boleh difahami.

### Peraturan finfluencer (R1-2025 — penambahan baru)

- Pengiklan secara sukarela (termasuk finfluencer) mesti mematuhi semua prinsip dan peraturan kandungan.
- Mesti mendedahkan manfaat langsung atau tidak langsung.
- Hashtag `#advertisement`, `#sponsored`, `#paidpartnership` dibenarkan jika pendedahan jelas dan mudah difahami.
- Pempengaruh yang mempromosi platform tidak berlesen SC akan melanggar §5.06 dan boleh didakwa membantu aktiviti tidak berlesen di bawah CMSA.

### Pihak ketiga & akauntabiliti penyelia (SC §6, §7)

- Lembaga pengarah pengiklan mesti memastikan kawalan / dasar / prosedur (CPP) berkesan.
- Pengiklan kekal bertanggungjawab terhadap pihak ketiga.
- Penyeliaan: rekod identiti pihak ketiga, akaun media sosial, semakan kandungan sebelum penerbitan, pemantauan berterusan.
- Untuk organisasi UTC: **Pengedar (Distributor)** ialah penyelia yang bertanggungjawab di bawah §7.02 SC + ¶3.19 FIMM CoE.

### Konsistensi dengan dokumen pendedahan (SC §8.02–§8.04)

Setiap iklan mesti konsisten dengan Prospektus Induk / PHS. Iklan mesti mengarahkan pelabur ke dokumen pendedahan, menyatakan lokasi, dan tarikh pengeluaran.

### Sekatan tajuk profesional

UTC + PRS Consultant berlesen **TIDAK BOLEH** gunakan:
- "Financial Planner" / "Perancang Kewangan" — memerlukan CMSRL.
- "Financial Advisor" / "Penasihat Kewangan" — sama.
- "Investment Advisor" / "Investment Consultant".

Tajuk yang dibenarkan: "Public Mutual Unit Trust Consultant", "PRS Consultant", "UTC", "Orang Berdaftar (FIMM)".

### Penyimpanan rekod & dasar dalaman (FAQ Q14)

Dasar bertulis mesti merangkumi:
1. Proses kelulusan dan pelaporan dalaman.
2. Kriteria penilaian pematuhan.
3. Penyimpanan rekod (iklan, penilaian kesesuaian, jejak kelulusan).

### Penguatkuasaan & preseden amaran

Garis panduan dikeluarkan di bawah seksyen 377 CMSA. Pelanggaran boleh menjurus kepada:
- Tindakan pentadbiran SC (seksyen 354 CMSA), teguran awam.
- Pendakwaan CMSA (mis. §369 — penyataan palsu/mengelirukan; §370 — bersubahat).
- Tindakan disiplin FIMM di bawah Bab 6 Peraturan Berkonsolidasi FIMM.

Contoh amaran daripada FAQ:
- **Contoh 7:** Risiko hanya didedahkan dalam kapsyen, bukan badan video — tidak patuh.
- **Contoh 8:** Risiko tersembunyi di sebalik "…more" Instagram — tidak patuh.
- **Contoh 9:** Pempengaruh mempromosi broker SG yang tidak dilesenkan SC — pelanggaran §5.06 + jenayah bersubahat CMSA.

### Soalan terbuka

- Perkongsian semula iklan korporat Public Mutual: tetap iklan; tambah penafian / kelayakan perunding sendiri agar tiga tonggak SC kekal jelas.
- Garis halus pendidikan vs promosi: "buy now" / nama dana tertentu + cadangan = iklan; literasi am = di luar skop.
- Hashtag `#advertisement` tidak mencukupi jika seluruh siaran masih kelihatan editorial — pendedahan mesti jelas kepada **khalayak sasaran**.
