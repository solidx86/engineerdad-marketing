### Why these surveys are proof

These are proprietary first-party data. Aggregate results can be cited as *"EngineerDad survey of N Malaysians, [date]"* — which is itself a credibility marker. The survey-driven hook ("83% of you fear losing money") is one of the highest-engaging IG opening lines in the archive.

The surveys also generate persona insight. Every quarter's results help the Brain refine which personas the closed loop is actually serving — and where the budget bucket should pivot.

### Citation discipline

When citing a survey statistic in any creative:
- Always state the sample size if known.
- Always state the date or recency band ("recent survey of 223 Malaysians").
- Never round up the sample (use 223, not "over 200" — the audience is more skeptical of vague numbers than precise ones).
- Never project the sample to a national claim ("83% of *all* Malaysians" — wrong; "83% of *the 223 Malaysian professionals surveyed*" — correct).
