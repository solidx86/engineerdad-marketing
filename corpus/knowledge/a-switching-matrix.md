# Switching: How PMB's Switch Facility Works, and the Double-charge Trap

> Figures sourced from Master Prospectus 1 of Public Series of Funds dated 28 August 2023 (with 1st Supplemental dated 3 December 2025), pp. 151–152 and p. 157. Switching fees for specific funds are also set out in each fund's Product Highlight Sheet (PHS). Verify the current PHS for the specific fund pair before use in campaign copy or client advice, as fees may be amended by supplemental prospectus. Nothing here constitutes personalised advice.

## English

### What switching is — and what it is not

A **switch** is a PMB-facility instruction to move some or all of a holding from Fund A into Fund B within the same transaction. It is NOT simply "I sold one fund and bought another" — the switch facility exists precisely because doing it the manual way (redeem + reinvest) triggers the full sales charge on the reinvestment leg. The switch facility is the mechanism that avoids that trap.

### How the prospectus frames switching

The PMB switching matrix is not framed as "intra-family vs cross-family". It is framed by two variables:

1. **The load category of the switch-out units** — whether those units were acquired with a sales charge of ≥3% (loaded), <3% (partial-load), or via a bond fund or money-market fund (each with their own category).
2. **The holding period** — whether the switch is made within 90 days, or after 90 days, from the date of purchase or last switch into that fund.

The switch facility operates across PMB's fund range (subject to eligibility). Moving from a PMB fund to a non-PMB fund is not a switch — it is a manual redeem-plus-subscribe on a different platform; the PMB switch facility does not apply.

### The switching charge matrix (pp. 151–152)

**Within 90 days** from the date of purchase or switching of units into the switch-out fund:

| Switch-out unit type | → Equity / Mixed Asset / Balanced fund | → Bond fund | → Money Market fund |
|---|---|---|---|
| Equity/Mixed/Balanced — loaded units (SC ≥ 3%) | Up to 0.75%* | Up to 0.75%* | Up to 0.75%* |
| Equity/Mixed/Balanced — partial-load units (SC < 3%) | Up to 0.50%* | Up to 0.50%* | Up to 0.50%* |
| Bond — loaded units (SC > 0.50%) | Up to 0.25%* | Up to 0.25%* | Up to 0.25%* |
| Bond — partial-load units (SC ≤ 0.50%) | Up to 0.50%* | Up to 0.50%* | Up to 0.25%* |
| Bond — 1%-load units (SC > 0.50%) | Sales charge up to 4.00% | Up to 0.25%* | Up to 0.25%* |
| Bond — low-load units (SC ≤ 0.50%) | Sales charge up to 4.75% | Sales charge up to 0.75% | Up to 0.25%* |
| Money Market — loaded units | Nil | Nil | Nil |
| Money Market — partial-load units | Up to 0.50%* | Up to 0.50%* | Nil |
| Money Market — 1%-load units | Sales charge up to 4.00% | Nil | Nil |
| Money Market — low-load units | Sales charge up to 4.75% | Sales charge up to 0.75% | Nil |
| Money Market — zero-load units | Sales charge up to 5.00% | Sales charge up to 1.00% | Nil |

**After 90 days** from the date of purchase or switching of units into the switch-out fund:

| Switch-out unit type | → Equity / Mixed Asset / Balanced fund | → Bond fund | → Money Market fund |
|---|---|---|---|
| Equity/Mixed/Balanced — loaded units (SC ≥ 3%) | Up to RM50 | Up to RM50 | Nil |
| Equity/Mixed/Balanced — partial-load units (SC < 3%) | Up to 0.50%* | Up to 0.50%* | Nil |
| Bond — loaded units | Up to RM50 | Up to RM50 | Nil |
| Bond — partial-load units | Up to 0.50%* | Up to 0.50%* | Nil |
| Bond — 1%-load units | Sales charge up to 4.00% | Up to RM50 | Nil |
| Bond — low-load units | Sales charge up to 4.75% | Sales charge up to 0.75% | Nil |
| Money Market — loaded units | Nil | Nil | Nil |
| Money Market — partial-load units | Up to 0.50%* | Up to 0.50%* | Nil |
| Money Market — 1%-load units | Sales charge up to 4.00% | Nil | Nil |
| Money Market — low-load units | Sales charge up to 4.75% | Sales charge up to 0.75% | Nil |
| Money Market — zero-load units | Sales charge up to 5.00% | Sales charge up to 1.00% | Nil |

\* Subject to a minimum of RM50, whichever is the higher.

**Definitions (from prospectus footnotes):**
- *Loaded units* — units which incurred a sales charge of 3% or more through investments in equity/mixed asset/balanced funds.
- *Partial-load units* — units which incurred a sales charge of less than 3% through investments in equity/mixed asset/balanced funds.
- *1%-load units* — units which incurred a sales charge of above 0.50% through investments in bond funds.
- *Low-load units* — units which incurred a sales charge of 0.50% or less through investments in bond funds.
- *Zero-load units* — units of money market funds which have not incurred any sales charge.

Minimum units for switching: 1,000 units (Master Prospectus p. 159).

### The "redeem-then-rebuy pays the sales charge" trap

This is the single most expensive mechanical mistake in unit trust portfolio management:

1. Investor holds RM50,000 in Fund A (equity, sales charge up to 5.0% of NAV per unit).
2. Wants to move to Fund B (equity, sales charge up to 5.0% of NAV per unit).
3. Redeems Fund A → receives ~RM50,000 proceeds (no redemption fee in most PMB equity funds).
4. Subscribes to Fund B with the RM50,000 proceeds → sales charge computed as: RM50,000 × 5/105 = **RM2,380.95 charged**, RM47,619.05 invested into Fund B.

If the investor had used the PMB switch facility instead (equity loaded units, after 90 days):
- Switch fee: **up to RM50** (fixed flat cap, per the prospectus switching matrix, pp. 151–152).
- Within 90 days: **up to 0.75%** of switched amount, subject to a minimum of RM50 — on RM50,000 that is up to RM375.
- Result: materially more of the RM50,000 reaches Fund B's unit allocation.

**The contrast:** redeem-then-resubscribe costs RM2,380.95 in sales charge; switching after 90 days costs up to RM50. The difference — up to RM2,330.95 — is gone before a single unit is allocated in the new fund.

**The trap is that "selling and rebuying" feels like the same economic action — but it isn't when a new sales charge is triggered.**

### Four things most investors don't know

- **Using the redeem-then-reinvest route instead of the switch facility can cost thousands of ringgit on a large position.** On a RM50,000 equity-to-equity move after 90 days, the unnecessary sales charge is RM2,380.95 versus a switch fee of up to RM50 — a difference of up to RM2,330.95 gone before compounding begins.
- **The switch fee depends on the age of the holding, not just the fund type.** The same loaded-unit equity-to-equity switch costs up to 0.75% (min RM50) within 90 days and up to RM50 flat after 90 days. Timing the switch to after the 90-day window cuts the maximum fee from a percentage-based charge to a flat RM50 cap.
- **Forward NAV applies on BOTH legs of a switch.** The redemption from Fund A is priced at Fund A's next NAV after the cut-off; the purchase of Fund B is priced at Fund B's next NAV after the cut-off. If Fund A and Fund B have different cut-off times, the two legs may price on different business days — the investor bears this timing risk.
- **A switch is operationally a redemption followed by a purchase — two valuation events.** The two legs do not necessarily execute at the same NAV or even on the same calendar day. A volatile market day between the two valuation points affects the effective conversion rate even if no explicit "conversion fee" is charged.

### Practical implications for portfolio rebalancing

For a client whose risk profile has shifted (e.g. from equity-heavy to balanced as retirement approaches), a structured rebalance using the PMB switch facility — rather than a series of redemptions and fresh subscriptions — can preserve several thousand ringgit in sales charges on a mature portfolio. The correct question before any rebalance is: "Does the switch facility apply to these two funds? What is the switching fee for my unit load category? Are the units older or younger than 90 days?" — not "What is the sales charge on the new fund?"

*Framing:* The switching-matrix conversation is the fastest way to demonstrate that fee literacy matters in practice. Most clients have never been told the switch facility exists, let alone that failing to use it is the equivalent of voluntarily paying a 5.0% entry charge on their own portfolio rebalance.
