# Rebalancing a PMB Portfolio Without Re-Paying Sales Charges

> Figures reflect publicly published PMB factsheets as of 2026-05-31; fund-specific values change quarterly — verify against the current factsheet. Not personalised advice.

## English

### The core problem: conventional rebalancing triggers a fresh sales charge

On a five- or ten-year horizon, a parent's PMB portfolio will drift from its original asset allocation as equity funds outperform bond funds in bull markets, or as a child's education timeline shortens and risk appetite should reduce. The instinct is to "sell the overweight fund and buy the underweight fund" — a textbook rebalance. In a standard brokerage or exchange context, this would cost trading commissions. In the PMB unit trust context, it costs something much larger: **a new sales charge on the reinvested amount**.

At a 5.0% equity sales charge ceiling (computed via the 5/105 method: RM50,000 × 5/105 = **RM2,380.95**), a RM50,000 rebalance from one equity fund to another costs RM2,380.95 in sales charges alone — before any consideration of market timing. Over a 20-year accumulation plan, a parent might rebalance three to five times; paying RM2,380.95+ each time is a significant, avoidable drag.

---

### The switch facility: the legal intra-family route

PMB's **switch facility** allows an investor to move holdings from Fund A to Fund B within the PMB fund family using a single switch instruction, without triggering a fresh sales charge. The mechanism is documented fully in `a-switching-matrix.md`; the key points for a rebalancing context are:

1. A switch is operationally a redemption from Fund A and a subscription to Fund B — but the subscription is made under the switch facility terms, which apply a **switching fee** (verify current PHS; typically far below the full sales charge, often near zero between many eligible PMB fund pairs) rather than the full sales charge.
2. Both legs of the switch price at forward NAV — the redemption from Fund A at Fund A's next valuation NAV, the subscription to Fund B at Fund B's next valuation NAV.
3. The switch facility is available only within the PMB fund family. Moving from a PMB fund to a non-PMB fund is a manual redeem-then-buy and incurs the full new-fund sales charge.

**The investor who redeems Fund A and re-subscribes to Fund B independently — rather than using the switch facility — pays the full sales charge on Fund B.** This is the single most expensive mechanical mistake in PMB portfolio management.

### Applying the switch facility to a children's fund portfolio

A typical EngineerDad client accumulates across two sleeves: a **children's education fund** (PMB unit trusts, equity-heavy in early years) and a **PRS sleeve** (for the parent's own retirement, benefiting from the RM3,000 PRS tax relief). Rebalancing each sleeve uses different mechanics.

**Children's education fund (unit trust sleeve):**

- As the education timeline shortens (e.g., child moves from age 5 to age 13, leaving 5 years to first year university fees), the appropriate move is typically equity → balanced or equity → bond/fixed-income to reduce volatility.
- Using the switch facility: submit a switch instruction from the equity fund to the balanced or bond fund. Pay the switching fee (verify PHS; typically low). No fresh equity sales charge.
- Tax note: There is no capital gains tax on unit trust transactions in Malaysia as of 2026-05-31. A switch does not create a taxable event for individual investors.

**PRS sleeve:**

- PRS funds offer intra-PRS switching — moving between the growth/moderate/conservative sub-funds within the same PRS provider (Public Mutual PRS) — typically at zero switch fee for the first switch per calendar year. Subsequent switches may carry a fee; verify the current PRS PHS.
- Switching from one PRS provider to another (cross-provider PRS transfer) follows a separate process under PPA guidelines and is subject to different terms — this is not the same as using the PMB switch facility.
- PRS fund switches do not affect the accumulated PRS balance's eligibility for the RM3,000 p.a. income tax relief. The relief is on contributions, not on intra-fund movements.

### When the switch facility does not eliminate all costs

The switch facility substantially reduces rebalancing cost, but three residual costs remain:

1. **The switching fee itself:** per the PMB Master Prospectus, for loaded equity units: **within 90 days** of original purchase, up to 0.75% of amount switched (minimum RM50); **after 90 days**, up to RM50 flat. On a RM50,000 switch: within-90-day maximum is RM375; after-90-day maximum is RM50 — both materially less than a 5.0% re-subscription charge of RM2,380.95 (5/105 method), but not zero.
2. **Bid-ask NAV timing risk:** the two legs of a switch price independently. If Fund A and Fund B have different dealing cut-off times, the two NAVs may apply on different business days. In a volatile market, the conversion rate between the two funds is not locked at the time the switch instruction is submitted.
3. **Cross-fund-family switches are impossible via the switch facility:** moving from a PMB fund to any non-PMB fund (e.g., CIMB Principal, Kenanga, Eastspring) requires a manual redemption and new subscription, with full new-fund sales charges.

### A practical rebalancing sequence for a PMB parent portfolio

1. **Audit current allocation** — export a statement, calculate the current split by asset class and fund.
2. **Identify the drift** — compare current vs target allocation (e.g., target 70% equity / 30% bond, actual 82% equity / 18% bond after a bull-market run).
3. **Check switch eligibility** — confirm both the source and target funds are within the PMB family and that the switch facility applies between them. Consult the current PHS or the consultant.
4. **Calculate the switch amount** — the amount to shift from the equity fund to bring the allocation back to target. Example: a RM200,000 portfolio at 82/18 needs to shift RM24,000 from equity to bond to reach 70/30.
5. **Submit a switch instruction**, not a redemption + new subscription. Confirm the switching fee with the consultant before submitting.
6. **Document the switch** — record the date, NAVs applied on both legs, units switched, and effective conversion rate. This creates an audit trail for future rebalances.

### Four things most investors don't know about rebalancing PMB portfolios

- **Using the redeem-then-reinvest route instead of the switch facility can cost thousands of ringgit on a mature portfolio.** On a RM50,000 equity-to-equity switch at 5.0% sales charge (5/105 method), the avoidable cost is RM2,380.95 — funds that could remain invested.
- **The switch facility only works intra-family.** A parent who holds both PMB and non-PMB funds cannot use the PMB switch facility to move between them. The switch facility's cost savings apply strictly within the PMB fund range.
- **PRS intra-fund switching is governed by PPA rules, not the PMB switch facility terms.** The first switch per year within a PRS sub-fund range is typically free; subsequent switches may carry fees; cross-provider PRS transfers follow a separate PPA process. These are three different mechanisms that are often conflated.
- **A portfolio rebalance is not the same as a redemption.** Using the switch facility preserves the accumulated investment — the capital moves from Fund A to Fund B without passing through the investor's bank account, without triggering a new sales charge, and without creating a tax event. It is purely a portfolio allocation change.

*Framing:* The switch facility is one of the most financially significant features of the PMB platform — and one of the least explained. A consultant who proactively walks a client through the switch-not-redeem mechanic demonstrates fee literacy and genuine long-term orientation. The client who learns this for the first time at the point of rebalance typically asks: "Why didn't you tell me this five years ago?"
