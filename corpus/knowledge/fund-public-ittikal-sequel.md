### Taxonomy notes

PITSEQ is the representative **large domestic Shariah equity** entry in the fund family. As a Shariah fund, holdings are restricted to SC-approved securities — financial services exposure is limited to Shariah-compliant instruments (conventional banking stocks are excluded). The communications and utilities weighting reflects the composition of the Shariah-compliant Malaysian equity universe, where telecommunications and infrastructure names tend to have larger index presence after conventional banking is removed. See `c-shariah-vs-conventional.md` for a full discussion of how the Shariah screen affects portfolio construction.
