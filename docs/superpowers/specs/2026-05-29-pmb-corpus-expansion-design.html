PMB Corpus Expansion — Design Spec

2026-05-29 · Topic: deeper-than-DCA technical content for the /corpus knowledge base

1. Purpose

Expand /corpus with PMB-specific technical knowledge artifacts that go materially deeper than the saturated "invest early / DCA" register. Corpus entries are raw knowledge units, not finished articles. Downstream agents (brain, brief-writer, content-writer, creative-director) compose articles, hooks, scripts, and creative variants from these primitives on demand.

Audience for the eventual surfaced content is mixed: already-investing PMB holders looking to optimize, plus analytical/skeptical Malaysian parents (engineers, accountants) who reject basic content because it feels patronizing. Authority-led — designed to anchor GEO/LLM answers and slice into short-form hooks.

2. Scope

Three knowledge clusters plus a transverse primitives layer. PMB-specific (not generic UT/PRS) — the moat is in the instantiation. Bilingual EN + BM (parallel registers, not translations) per ADR-010.

3. Clusters

Cluster A — Mechanics

The stuff every PMB holder thinks they understand but doesn't. High "I didn't know that" density. Strongest moat layer.

  • PMB fee schedule — full table: sales charge, switching fee, redemption fee, annual management fee per fund family
  • PMB switching matrix — which funds can swap intra-family vs cross-family without re-charging sales fee; the 6% trap of redeem-then-reinvest
  • Sales-charge math models — 5.5% upfront vs 1.8% annual over 5/10/20-year horizons; break-even tables
  • NAV cutoff + T+ settlement reference — same-day vs next-day pricing rules
  • Distribution mechanics — ex-date, payment date, NAV impact, auto-reinvest pricing rule
  • Trust deed + prospectus excerpt library — bookmarked clauses on charges, switching, suspension, force majeure
  • Bid-offer spread retirement — what replaced it, why statements still look confusing

Cluster B — Tax / PRS Optimization

Compound multi-year tax mechanics. Tax-season GEO surface. Parents Google this every March.

  • Malaysia tax-relief stacking matrix — PRS / EPF i-Saraan / SSPN / life insurance / education / medical; caps, interactions, LHDN code references
  • PRS pre-55 withdrawal mechanics — 8% penalty, exemptions (medical / housing / death), opportunity-cost frame
  • PRS default option vs self-select — regulatory rules by age band, performance comparison vs Public Mutual PRS series
  • PRS estate-planning interaction — nomination vs hibah vs faraid for PRS + UT accounts
  • Self-employed contribution math — EPF voluntary vs PRS vs i-Saraan; income-bracket thresholds where each wins

Cluster C — Portfolio Construction

For the analytical parent who actually reads factsheets. Hardest to write, highest authority signal.

  • Per-fund factsheet archive — last 4 quarters per PMB fund Shoo actively recommends
  • PMB fund-family taxonomy — equity / balanced / fixed income / Shariah / regional, mapped to PMB's actual classification
  • Risk-metric glossary — Sharpe, std dev, beta, max drawdown — tied to PMB statement language
  • Shariah vs conventional pairs — tracking error, fee differential, structural advantages
  • Currency exposure breakdown — hidden MYR / USD / regional bets inside global PMB funds
  • Rebalancing without triggering sales charges — the legal intra-family path

Transverse — Primitives Layer

Unlocks every cluster. Front-load this.

  • Bilingual glossary (EN + BM) — NAV, bid-offer, distribution, switching, redemption, dividend yield, units, etc.
  • Compliance phrase bank — SC / FIMM disclaimer requirements, claimable vs non-claimable performance language
  • Proof asset index — registry of factsheets, screenshots, prospectus clauses available for citation

4. Artifact Shape

Each corpus entry is a structured, citable, evergreen knowledge unit.

FieldPurpose
titleConcise descriptor
clustermechanics | tax | portfolio | primitive
granularityfund (per-PMB-fund instance) or concept (mechanic, rule, definition). Both are first-class.
source_typepublic (prospectus, factsheet, LHDN, FIMM, SC) or synthesized (plausible scenario patterns authored from industry knowledge + public UT mechanics; not real client cases). Both are first-class; synthesized entries are tagged so downstream content framing can enforce "scenario / example / hypothetical" language and never imply a real client.
source_refCitation — URL, doc + page, prospectus revision date, or "synthesized 2026-05-29"
verified_atDate last verified against source. Drives staleness checks.
body_en / body_msParallel EN + BM bodies (not literal translations — separate registers; BM leans Muslim-parent framing, EN leans technical/analytical)
relatedLinks to other corpus entry IDs
Why both granularities: concept-level entries (e.g., "switching fee mechanics") are reusable across all funds; fund-level entries (e.g., "PMITTF Q1 2026 factsheet") are the proof layer that grounds the concept. Brain composes by pulling 1 concept + N fund instances.
Why synthesized-source is first-class: Plausible scenario patterns ("a holder who redeemed PMITTF to buy PMGF paid the sales charge twice") carry the strongest hook punch and are unreplicable by competitors who only restate the concept. They are not real client cases and must never be framed as such. Source tag drives mandatory downstream framing — content-writer wraps them as "imagine / scenario / example" only.

6. Hook Extraction Rule (downstream contract)

Not part of corpus ingestion itself, but a quality bar for entries: each concept-level entry should contain at least 4 standalone "I didn't know that" facts — single-claim, single-stat, or single-mechanic surprises. If an entry doesn't yield 4, it isn't technical enough; deepen it or merge with a sibling. This makes content-writer's ≥30-hook bank achievable.

7. Proof Discipline

Every entry must carry at least one of: a real PMB factsheet reference, an actual fund code worked example, or a prospectus / trust-deed clause citation. No generic "unit trusts in general…" hedging. No stock-photo proof. This is the moat — competitors can write the concept; they can't write the PMB-specific instantiation with verifiable citation.

8. Out of Scope

  • Article briefs, hook banks, scripts, carousels, creatives — these are outputs the OS surfaces from corpus; not part of this spec
  • Generic Malaysia UT/PRS content not tied to PMB
  • Content cadence / publishing calendar — separate scheduling concern
  • Corpus storage schema changes (assume current /corpus entry shape accommodates the fields above; spec a schema change separately if not)

9. Prerequisite — Schema Extension (verified gap)

Current /corpus shape (per packages/corpus/src/tools.ts):

  • Fixed Scope enum: compliance | courses | proof
  • Markdown files indexed by BM25; language sectioned by ## English / ## Bahasa Malaysia headers
  • Frontmatter convention exists only on proof/*.md (persona, quote, attribution, permission_status)
  • No cluster, granularity, or source_type fields anywhere

Required extension (chosen approach — least disruption):

  • Keep the three existing scopes as-is (functional roles unchanged)
  • Extend the frontmatter convention universally: every corpus markdown file may carry cluster, granularity, source_type, source_ref, verified_at, related
  • Update the reindex pipeline to parse these fields and attach to each Chunk
  • Extend SearchInput with optional filters (cluster?, granularity?, source_type?) — post-filter on the loaded chunk metadata, same pattern as the existing scope / lang filter at tools.ts:45
  • New entries land in a new top-level dir (e.g. corpus/knowledge/) — or extend the Scope enum to add a knowledge scope. Decide in the implementation plan.

This is a separate ADR + migration (corpus package + corpus MCP). It must land before ingestion phase 1 of this spec begins.

10. Resolved Decisions

  • Authoring: Claude authors all corpus entries directly. public entries cite real documents (PMB prospectus, factsheets, LHDN, FIMM, SC). synthesized entries are plausible scenario patterns authored from industry knowledge + public UT mechanics, never claimed as real cases.
  • Granularity: Both concept and fund entries are first-class.
  • Source types: public and synthesized only. No consultant tag — Claude has no field data, mislabeling would seed compliance risk.
  • Volume target: Phase 1 primitives + Cluster A ≈ 15 entries; Cluster B ≈ 10; Cluster C ≈ 15 (factsheet-heavy, quick once template exists). ~40 entries total. Plus/minus ±20% acceptable.
  • Language parity: EN ships first per entry; BM follows as a separate pass and does not block first commit. Frontmatter lang_status tracks en_only | both.
  • Compliance review: Trust + spot-check for both public and synthesized. No automated source verification. Shoo reviews entries in the PR before merge.
  • Staleness policy: Out of scope for this spec.
