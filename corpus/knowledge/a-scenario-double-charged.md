# The Double-Charge Trap: An Illustrative Switching Scenario

> The scenario (the parent, the RM50,000 amount) is illustrative — not a real client case, and not personalised advice. **The fee rates, however, are factual**: the 5.0% equity sales charge (5/105 method, PMB Master Prospectus) and the switching-charge schedule (PMB Product Highlights Sheet). Verify current rates in the relevant PHS before any client conversation.

## English

### Setting the scene

Imagine a parent who has held a PMB equity fund — call it Fund A — for several years and now wants to shift the money into a different PMB equity fund, Fund B, because their outlook on the sector has changed. The intention is straightforward: move the holding from one fund to another.

There are two paths to this outcome. The parent takes Path A first — and only discovers Path B after the fact.

### Path A — Redeem and resubscribe (the expensive route)

Suppose the holding in Fund A is worth RM50,000 at the time of the move. The parent redeems the full amount and receives the proceeds (PMB equity funds carry no redemption fee, so the full RM50,000 lands in their account). They then submit a fresh subscription into Fund B.

Here is what happens on the subscription:

| Assumption | Value |
|---|---|
| Amount reinvested into Fund B | RM50,000 |
| Sales charge rate — equity funds | up to 5.0% (5/105 method; ceiling from Master Prospectus) |
| Sales charge on this transaction | **RM2,380.95** (50,000 × 5/105) |
| Amount actually entering Fund B | **RM47,619.05** |

The parent paid RM2,380.95 in sales charges — not because they bought a new position with fresh savings, but simply because they moved an existing holding from one fund to another.

What makes this doubly frustrating: they almost certainly paid a similar sales charge when they first subscribed to Fund A years ago. That original entry cost has long been absorbed. But Path A triggers the clock again — a full 5.0% entry cost (via 5/105 method) on money that was already inside the PMB system.

### Path B — Use the PMB switch facility (the low-cost route)

Now consider the same parent using the PMB switch facility instead: a single switch instruction from Fund A to Fund B, with no redeem-and-resubscribe.

PMB's switching charge is set by three things — how long the units were held (within vs after 90 days), whether they are **loaded** (original sales charge of 3% or more) or **partial-load** (less than 3%), and the recipient fund type:

| Switch-out | → Equity / Mixed Asset / Balanced | → Bond | → Money Market |
|---|---|---|---|
| Within 90 days — loaded | up to 0.75%* | up to 0.75%* | up to 0.75%* |
| Within 90 days — partial-load | up to 0.50%* | up to 0.50%* | up to 0.50%* |
| After 90 days — loaded | **up to RM50** | up to RM50 | Nil |
| After 90 days — partial-load | up to 0.50%* | up to 0.50%* | Nil |

\* Percentage charges are subject to a **minimum of RM50**, whichever is higher. Loaded = units that incurred a sales charge of ≥3%; partial-load = <3%. (Source: PMB switching-charge schedule, Product Highlights Sheet.)

In this scenario the parent held Fund A for several years (**after 90 days**) and the units are **loaded** (the original equity subscription carried a ≥3% sales charge). Switching into another equity fund therefore lands in the *after-90-days / loaded / equity* cell:

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Amount being switched | RM50,000 |
| Switching charge (after 90 days, loaded units → equity) | **up to RM50** (flat cap) |
| Amount reaching Fund B | **≥ RM49,950** |

The difference between Path A and Path B on this RM50,000 move: **RM2,380.95 − RM50 = RM2,330.95**. That is money that stays in the portfolio and compounds in Fund B rather than leaving the system as a fee.

*Note the timing edge: had the parent switched **within 90 days** of subscribing, the charge would be up to 0.75% (min RM50) ≈ RM375 on RM50,000 — still far below Path A, but the flat RM50 cap only applies after the 90-day mark.*

### Why this happens without anyone noticing

Consider the scenario from the parent's perspective. They submit a redemption form. The money arrives in their bank account. They submit a subscription form for Fund B. Each step feels like a clean, straightforward instruction — and it is. Nothing about the process signals that they have just volunteered to pay a 5.0% entry cost (via 5/105 method) on money that was already invested.

The sales charge appears as a standard line on the Fund B contract note. If the parent does not compare it against what a switch would have cost, the RM2,380.95 disappears invisibly — absorbed into what looks like a normal transaction.

This is distinct from paying a sales charge on new money coming in from outside the investment system. That cost buys access to a fund from zero. The double-charge trap costs the same rate on money that was already inside PMB's fund range, doing work, and simply needed to move.

### The four things to take from this scenario

- **Redeeming and resubscribing within the same fund family is not the same as switching** — one triggers a full sales charge on the reinvestment leg; the other uses a facility specifically designed to move holdings at a fraction of that cost.
- **On a RM50,000 equity-to-equity move held past 90 days, the cost difference between Path A and Path B is RM2,330.95** (RM2,380.95 sales charge vs the RM50 switch-charge cap) — a meaningful amount that disappears before a single unit is allocated in the new fund.
- **The trap is invisible in the transaction flow.** Each step — redeem, receive proceeds, subscribe — feels like routine administration. The fee comparison only surfaces if you know to look for it before submitting the forms.
- **The switch facility exists precisely for this situation.** PMB offers an intra-family switch facility so investors can restructure their holdings without re-paying full entry costs. Not knowing it exists is the most expensive form of fund literacy gap in routine portfolio maintenance.
- **Cross-family moves are a legitimate exception.** If the destination is a non-PMB fund, there is no switch facility to use — the full sales charge on the new fund is unavoidable and expected. The double-charge trap specifically applies to moves within the PMB fund range where the switch facility is available.
