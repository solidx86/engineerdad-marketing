# PRS Withdrawal Mechanics

> Tax figures reflect publicly published LHDN/PPA schedules as of 2026-05-31; tax rules change annually — confirm the active year with LHDN and consult a tax adviser. Not personalised tax advice. The 8% pre-retirement withdrawal tax and permitted exemption categories are drawn from publicly published PPA rules; the exact tax treatment may depend on individual circumstances — confirm with LHDN and a tax adviser.

## English

### The sub-account structure

Every PRS member account is divided into two sub-accounts:

| Sub-account | Proportion of contribution | Withdrawal rules |
|---|---|---|
| Account A (Retirement) | 70% of each contribution | Accessible only at age 55, permanent departure from Malaysia, or death/total permanent disability |
| Account B (Pre-retirement) | 30% of each contribution | Subject to limited pre-retirement withdrawal (once per year, with conditions) |

This split is mandatory across all PRS providers, including Public Mutual's PRS funds. It is not configurable by the member.

### Four facts that matter for pre-retirement withdrawal

- **A pre-retirement withdrawal from Account B incurs an 8% tax charge on the amount withdrawn.** This is not an administrative fee — it is a tax levied by LHDN under the Income Tax Act on qualifying pre-retirement distributions. A RM10,000 pre-retirement withdrawal nets RM9,200 after the 8% charge. The charge applies regardless of the member's marginal income tax rate.
- **The 8% charge is applied per withdrawal, not per year.** Only one Account B withdrawal is permitted per calendar year. The charge applies each time a qualifying withdrawal is made before retirement.
- **Certain categories are exempt from the 8% tax charge.** Confirmed permitted reasons for tax-exempt early access (ppa.my/prs-faq/, fetched 2026-05-31) include: (a) permanent departure from Malaysia, (b) total permanent disability / serious disease / mental disability, (c) attaining age 55, (d) **housing purposes** (purchase, construction, or loan reduction — confirmed exempt under PRS; separate from EPF housing withdrawal), and (e) **healthcare expenses** (specified illnesses for self and immediate family — confirmed exempt). Death of member (proceeds to estate/nominee) is also treated as a retirement withdrawal and is penalty-free. Consultants should verify the current full exemption list with PPA and LHDN, as exemption categories may be updated by regulation.
- **The opportunity cost of withdrawal compounds against the member.** A RM10,000 withdrawal at age 35 that would have compounded at an illustrative 7% p.a. until age 55 would have grown to approximately RM38,700 at retirement. The 8% tax charge is the immediate cost; the foregone compounding is the hidden cost. Both are borne by the same withdrawal decision.
- **Account A cannot be accessed pre-retirement under any circumstance except death, total permanent disability, or permanent departure.** This is a legal constraint, not a PRS provider policy. Members who expect full liquidity from their PRS account will be surprised to find 70% of their balance inaccessible until age 55.

### The retirement withdrawal path

From age 55 onward, the 8% penalty does not apply. Members can withdraw in lump sum, by instalment, or leave the balance invested and continue to grow tax-deferred. The PRS is designed as a long-run accumulation vehicle; the withdrawal mechanics are deliberately structured to penalise early access and reward patience.

*Framing:* The 8% withdrawal tax is usually the fact that surprises an investor who assumed PRS was "like EPF but with a tax break". The sub-account split and the exemption list together define the liquidity profile of PRS — and that profile is substantially less flexible than EPF's voluntary withdrawal windows. Knowing this prevents a client from contributing more to PRS than their liquidity position can absorb.
